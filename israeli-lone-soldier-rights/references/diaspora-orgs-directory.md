# Diaspora and Israeli NGO Directory for Lone Soldiers

These organizations operate independently of the IDF / MoD and fill gaps that the official benefits package does not cover. Route lone soldiers to them in addition to the official applications.

## Nefesh B'Nefesh + FIDF Lone Soldier Program (NBN+FIDF LSP)

- URL: https://www.nbn.org.il/nbnlsp/
- Operating model: joint program of Nefesh B'Nefesh and Friends of the IDF
- What they offer (verify current programs on the NBN site):
  - 24/7 hotline for lone soldier issues
  - Pre-aliyah and pre-enlistment guidance for olim
  - Advocacy with government agencies (Mas Hachnasa, Bituach Leumi, hachvana)
  - Errands Day events
  - Holiday meals and family hosting
  - Mental-health support tier (Resilience Center concept)
  - Post-discharge career placement programs
- Best for: olim from North America (NBN primary catchment), needs that span pre-aliyah through post-discharge

## Friends of the IDF (FIDF)

- URL: https://www.fidf.org/lone-soldier-support/
- Operating model: US-based NGO, runs Israel-side programs directly
- What they offer (verify current programs on the FIDF site):
  - Lone-soldier housing programs
  - Financial safety-net programs at the start of service
  - Annual Lone Soldier Fun Day events
  - Parent-reunion flight programs (co-funded with the IDF "מתגעגעים הביתה" flight benefit)
- Best for: olim and Israeli lone soldiers needing direct material support beyond NBN counseling

## Garin Tzabar

- URL: https://garintzabar.org/
- Operating model: aliyah-and-IDF preparation program by Tzofim (Israeli Scouts)
- What they offer (verify current numbers on garintzabar.org):
  - Pre-aliyah seminars across multiple regions (North America, Latin America, Western Europe, South Africa, Australia, Russian-speaking countries, current region list on the Garin Tzabar site)
  - Full-board kibbutz accommodation + ulpan during pre-army adaptation
  - Assigned mentor + host family
  - "Family for Life" alumni network (operating since 1991, multiple thousands of alumni)
- Eligibility: ages 18-24, high-school graduate, aliyah-eligible
- Best for: structured cohort experience before enlistment, lasting alumni network

## Lone Soldier Center in Memory of Michael Levin

- URL: https://www.lonesoldiercenter.com/
- Operating model: Israel-side independent NGO
- (Note: tzevetmikey.com is a SEPARATE organization, Tzevet Mikey / The Lone Soldier Empowerment Foundation, a pre-army training program, do not confuse the two)
- What they offer:
  - Apartments (subsidized for lone soldiers in major cities)
  - Communal meals
  - Holiday programs and hosting
  - Emergency assistance during service
  - Post-discharge community continuity
- Best for: ongoing community connection during and after service, especially for estranged Israeli lone soldiers (who NBN/FIDF less commonly serve)

## Mahal Program

- URL: http://www.mahal-idf-volunteers.org/
- Operating model: IDF program for non-Israeli Jewish volunteers
- What they offer (verify exact service-length and placement rates on current Mahal materials):
  - Tracks: Mahal Regular (commonly cited around 18 months), Mahal Hesder (religious-track, longer), Mahal Haredi (Netzach Yehuda combat)
  - Foreign-volunteer / tourist-status track (not olim)
  - Combat placement varies by track (Mahal Regular places across a wide range of positions; Mahal Haredi is mostly combat)
- Important distinction: Mahal soldiers are automatically recognized as lone soldiers during service, but their post-discharge package differs from oleh-track lone soldiers (no Israeli citizenship by default, no post-discharge benefits from hachvana the same way)
- Best for: non-Israeli Jews who want to serve without committing to permanent aliyah

## Aguda LeMaan HaChayal (Soldier's Welfare Association)

- Operating model: large Israeli NGO operating Beit HaChayal lodging network + ancillary services
- What they offer:
  - Free Beit HaChayal lodging on weekends, holidays, extended weekends across 7 cities (Kiryat Shmona, Tveriya, Yerushalayim, Haifa, Tel Aviv, Ramat Gan, Be'er Sheva)
  - Co-funder of the "מתגעגעים הביתה" free-flights program
  - Food vouchers and holiday vouchers
  - Subsidized dental + glasses + therapy not covered by Kupat Cholim
- Coordination: through unit Mashak Tash; coordinated with IDF benefits

## When to route to which org

| Soldier situation | Primary recommendation |
|---|---|
| North American oleh, pre-aliyah | NBN+FIDF LSP for full lifecycle support |
| Pre-army structured cohort experience | Garin Tzabar |
| Mid-service emergency or community need | Lone Soldier Center in Memory of Michael Levin |
| Foreign volunteer not committing to aliyah | Mahal |
| Weekend lodging, food vouchers, free flights | Aguda LeMaan HaChayal via Mashak Tash |
| Estranged Israeli lone soldier (no diaspora connection) | Lone Soldier Center + IDF Mashak Tash + Welfare Officer |
| Post-discharge career transition | NBN Atidim TAKEOFF + Garin Tzabar alumni network |
| Therapy / mental health during or after service | NBN+FIDF Resilience Center + `israeli-mental-health-navigator` |
