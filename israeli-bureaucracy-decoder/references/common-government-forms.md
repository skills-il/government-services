# Common Israeli Government Forms

An index of frequently encountered government forms, what they are for, who needs to file them, and when. Use this when a document references a form number or when advising which forms to file.

---

## Tax Authority Forms (טפסי רשות המסים)

### Income Tax Forms

| Form | Hebrew Name | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|-------------|---------|-----------|------|
| **101** | טופס 101 | Employee tax declaration: personal details, tax credits, and deductions for your employer | Every employee | When starting a new job, at the beginning of each tax year, or when personal circumstances change (marriage, child, new address) |
| **106** | טופס 106 | Annual employer tax certificate: summary of your salary and tax withheld during the year | Employer gives to employee | By March 31 of the following year |
| **135** | טופס 135 | Annual income tax return for individuals | Self-employed individuals and those required to file | By April 30 (paper) or May 31 (online) of the following year |
| **1301** | טופס 1301 | Annual income tax return (detailed, for those with business/self-employment income) | Self-employed, business owners, those with capital gains | By April 30 (paper) or May 31 (online) of the following year |
| **1214** | טופס 1214 | Tax refund request for salaried employees | Employees who overpaid tax | Within 6 years of the relevant tax year |
| **1227** | טופס 1227 | Capital gains tax report for real estate transactions | Anyone selling property | Within 30 days of the transaction |
| **119** | טופס 119 | Request for advance tax ruling | Anyone needing clarity on tax treatment before a transaction | Before the transaction takes place |
| **134** | טופס 134 | Self-assessment for individuals with income from capital, overseas, or other non-salary sources | Individuals with passive income | Annual, with the tax return |
| **1325** | טופס 1325 | Request for tax coordination (tium mas) when you have two or more employers | Employees with multiple jobs | When starting an additional job |
| **150** | טופס 150 | Objection to a tax assessment (hashagah) | Anyone who disagrees with their assessment | Within 30 days of receiving the assessment |

### VAT Forms

| Form | Hebrew Name | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|-------------|---------|-----------|------|
| **874** | טופס 874 | VAT registration as a licensed dealer (osek murshe) | New businesses above the exempt threshold | Before starting business activity |
| **821** | טופס 821 | Periodic VAT return | All VAT-registered businesses | Monthly or bi-monthly, by the 15th |
| **852** | טופס 852 | Annual VAT summary | VAT-registered businesses | By March 31 |

## National Insurance (Bituach Leumi) Forms (טפסי ביטוח לאומי)

| Form | Hebrew Name | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|-------------|---------|-----------|------|
| **BL/250** | בל/250 | Reserve duty compensation claim (dmei miluim) | Employers on behalf of employees who did reserve duty, or self-employed individuals | Within 60 days of completing reserve duty |
| **BL/283** | בל/283 | Work injury claim | Employees injured at work | As soon as possible after the injury |
| **BL/211** | בל/211 | Maternity allowance claim (dmei leida) | Pregnant women or new mothers | From week 34 of pregnancy, up to 12 months after birth |
| **BL/1500** | בל/1500 | General disability claim | Anyone who became disabled | As soon as the condition is stable |
| **BL/301** | בל/301 | Unemployment benefits claim (dmei avtala) | Recently unemployed individuals | Within 12 months of becoming unemployed |
| **BL/620** | בל/620 | Child allowance registration (when not automatic) | Parents of newborns (usually automatic, but sometimes requires manual filing) | After birth |
| **BL/500** | בל/500 | Old-age pension claim (kitzba zikna) | Individuals reaching retirement age | A few months before reaching retirement age |
| **BL/340** | בל/340 | Hostile action (terror) victim claim | Victims of terror attacks | As soon as possible |

## Municipality Forms (טפסים עירוניים)

| Form | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| **Arnona discount request** (בקשה להנחה בארנונה) | Apply for reduced arnona based on income, age, disability, new immigrant status, or other qualifying criteria | Property owners/renters meeting eligibility criteria | Annually, typically between January and March |
| **Property usage change notification** (הודעה על שינוי שימוש בנכס) | Notify the municipality that the use of your property changed (e.g., from residential to commercial) | Property owners | Within 30 days of the change |
| **Business license application** (בקשה לרישיון עסק) | Apply for a license to operate a business | Anyone opening or relocating a business | Before starting operations |
| **Building permit application** (בקשה להיתר בנייה) | Request permission to build or renovate | Property owners planning construction | Before starting any construction work |
| **Objection to arnona classification** (השגה על סיווג ארנונה) | Challenge how the municipality classified your property (which affects your rate) | Property owners who believe classification is wrong | Within 90 days of receiving the arnona bill |

## Interior Ministry / Population Authority Forms (טפסי משרד הפנים)

| Form | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| **ID card application** (בקשה לתעודת זהות) | Apply for or renew an Israeli ID card | Citizens at age 16, or when replacing a lost/damaged card | At age 16 or upon change of status |
| **Passport application** (בקשה לדרכון) | Apply for or renew an Israeli passport | Israeli citizens | When passport expires or is needed for the first time |
| **Name change request** (בקשה לשינוי שם) | Legally change your name | Israeli citizens | Anytime |
| **Visa extension request** (בקשה להארכת אשרה) | Extend a visa/residence permit | Non-citizens in Israel | Before the current visa expires |
| **Family reunification application** (בקשה לאיחוד משפחות) | Apply for a spouse or family member to receive residency | Israeli citizens/residents with foreign family members | Varies |

## Land Registry (Tabu) Forms (טפסי טאבו)

| Form | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| **Property transfer request** (בקשה להעברת זכויות) | Transfer property ownership from one person to another | Buyer and seller of property | After completing a real estate transaction |
| **Mortgage registration** (בקשה לרישום משכנתא) | Register a mortgage against a property | Banks/lending institutions | When a mortgage is taken |
| **Caveat registration** (בקשה לרישום הערת אזהרה) | Register a warning note on a property (e.g., during a purchase process) | Buyers, creditors | During property transactions or when establishing a legal claim |
| **Nesach Tabu request** (בקשה לנסח טאבו) | Request an official extract showing property ownership and encumbrances | Anyone (it is public information) | Available online at gov.il for a small fee |

## Court Forms (טפסי בית משפט)

| Form | Purpose | Who Files | When |
|------|---------|-----------|------|
| **Statement of claim** (כתב תביעה) | Initiate a lawsuit | The plaintiff (person suing) | When filing a case |
| **Statement of defense** (כתב הגנה) | Respond to a lawsuit | The defendant (person being sued) | Within 30 days of receiving the claim (60 days for the State) |
| **Small claims form** (טופס תביעה קטנה) | File a small claims case (up to 38,800 NIS) | Anyone suing for a small amount | When filing at Small Claims Court |
| **Fee exemption request** (בקשה לפטור מאגרה) | Request waiver of court filing fees due to financial hardship | Low-income plaintiffs | When filing the case |
| **Opposition to execution** (התנגדות לביצוע) | Challenge an enforcement/execution proceeding | Defendants in Hotzaa LaPoal proceedings | Within 20 days of receiving the warning (azhara) |

## Tips for Form Filing

1. **Always keep copies** of every form you submit, with the date stamp or receipt confirmation.
2. **Online filing** is available for many forms at gov.il, the Tax Authority portal (mof.gov.il), and Bituach Leumi portal (btl.gov.il). Online filing is generally faster and provides automatic confirmation.
3. **Deadlines matter.** Most government forms have strict deadlines. Late submissions may be accepted with penalties, or may not be accepted at all.
4. **Hebrew is required** for most government forms. Some forms are available in Arabic and English, but the Hebrew version is always the authoritative one.
5. **Notarization (notaryon)** is required for some forms, especially those involving property or powers of attorney. A notary in Israel is a licensed attorney ("notaryon").
6. **Apostille** is required for foreign documents being submitted to Israeli government bodies. Available at the Justice Ministry.
