# Survivor's allowance rate table (קצבת שאירים), effective 01.01.2026

All amounts are the current monthly base figures published by Bituach Leumi. They are updated
each year on 1 January by the index. Always re-verify the current-year figure before quoting an
amount to a survivor.

Source (widow/widower): https://www.btl.gov.il/benefits/Survivors_%20Insurance/shiuraihakizba/Pages/k.almanim.aspx
Source (orphans): https://www.btl.gov.il/benefits/Survivors_%20Insurance/shiuraihakizba/Pages/k.yatom.aspx
Source (seniority): https://www.btl.gov.il/benefits/Survivors_%20Insurance/shiuraihakizba/Pages/vetek.aspx
Effective date on the page: החל מ- 01.01.2026

## Widow / widower monthly base (amount set by age + number of children)

| Category | Monthly base (NIS) |
|---|---|
| Widow(er) aged 40-50, no children | 1,381 |
| Widow(er) aged 50+, no children | 1,838 |
| Widow(er) aged 80+ | 1,941 |
| Widow(er) with one child | 2,700 |
| Widow(er) with two children | 3,562 |
| Each additional child (beyond two) | +862 |
| Half the childless-50+ base (old-age-pension interaction) | 919 |

Notes:
- There is no cap on the number of eligible children ("אין הגבלה במספר הילדים הזכאים").
- The "with children" rows already fold in the child portion, so they do not depend on the
  widow(er)'s age band the way the childless rows do.
- A widow is entitled by law without an income test. A widower without dependent children is
  subject to an income test (see below).
- Old-age (vatik) pension interaction: a widow(er) entitled to both an old-age pension and a
  survivor's allowance is paid the old-age pension IN FULL plus HALF of the survivor's allowance
  (if they accrued the old-age qualifying period as an insured worker). 919 is specifically half
  of the childless-50+ base (half of 1,838); a widow(er) with children gets half of the higher
  with-children rate, so 919 is not a universal half-allowance figure. A widowed homemaker at the
  absolute pension age is not paid the extra half and instead chooses the higher of the two.

## Orphan monthly base (special circumstances)

Within a normal widow(er)+children family the child portions are inside the widow(er) rows above.
A stand-alone orphan allowance is paid in these cases:

| Category | Monthly base (NIS) |
|---|---|
| Orphan whose parent is not entitled, single child | 1,142 |
| Orphan whose parent is not entitled, each, when more than one | 862 |
| Orphan of both parents, per child | 2,284 |

## Seniority increment (תוספת ותק)

- 2% of the base allowance per full insurance year of the deceased, counted from the first
  insurance year (rule since January 2019).
- Maximum increment: 50% of the base allowance.
- A full insurance year = 12 insurance months (consecutive or not).
- Applies to the widow(er) base and to orphan-of-both-parents / parent-not-entitled orphan bases.

## Income test for a widower (מבחן הכנסות)

- A widower can be entitled if his total income is up to 7,848 NIS (from 01.01.2026).
- From income from work (employee or self-employed), pension, or a monthly income first paid at
  retirement age, deduct 2,093 NIS (from 01.01.2026) before the test.
- To apply the test, deduct the 2,093 NIS from qualifying income (work, pension, or a monthly
  income first paid at retirement age), then compare the remainder against the 7,848 NIS ceiling.

## Special survivors' allowance (גמלה מיוחדת לשאירים)

- Paid to the widow(er) / orphans of an Israeli resident who died but was not insured (immigrated
  after the set age).
- Rates equal the survivor's-allowance rates above, without the seniority increment.
- Claim form: 4506.

## Survivor grant (מענק שאירים), one-time

- For a widow(er) under 40 with no children (and certain income cases).
- Amount: 36 monthly survivor's allowances.
- Exception (monthly allowance instead of the grant): an under-40 childless widow(er) still gets
  the monthly allowance if she is pregnant with or later bears the deceased's child (treated as a
  widow(er) with a child, entitled from the date of death), and, in the work-related-death
  (dependents') track, if she was unable to support herself from work at the time of death (paid
  as if she were 50, regardless of age).

## Marriage grant (מענק נישואים) on remarriage, one-time

- Remarriage (or a new common-law relationship) terminates the survivor's allowance.
- Amount: 36 monthly allowances, paid in two installments (18 after the marriage, 18 about two
  years later).
- Continued instead if the new spouse is 60+ or cannot support himself with income below the
  7,848 NIS ceiling; reinstated if the widow(er) divorces (or begins divorce proceedings) within
  10 years of remarrying.

## Death grant (מענק פטירה), one-time

- 10,514 NIS (from 01.01.2026).
- To the spouse of a deceased who received old-age, income-support, general-disability,
  special-services, work-injury, survivors, or prisoners-of-Zion benefits; if no spouse, to the child.
- Usually paid automatically; the survivor's-allowance claim (form 410) already includes it.
