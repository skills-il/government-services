# Dmei Avtala Application Forms and Portals

All official forms, portals, and contact points for Israeli unemployment claims. Procedural windows are 2026 values; verify the current set on btl.gov.il/benefits/Unemployment before relying on them.

## Primary Portals

| Portal | URL | Purpose |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Sherut HaTaasuka (Employment Service) | https://www.taasuka.gov.il | Register as job seeker, attend reporting, job search |
| Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) | https://www.btl.gov.il | File unemployment claim, check status, appeal decisions |
| Government Login (zehut digitalit) | https://www.gov.il | Unified digital ID for all online services |

## Required Documents

Gather these before starting the online application:

1. **Teudat Zehut** (ID card) + sefach (appendix)
2. **Termination letter** (mikhtav piturin) signed by employer, stating the termination date
3. **Form 126** (tofes 126) from the employer for the current year. **Form 126 is the annual employer summary, NOT the monthly Form 100.** Bringing only Form 100 is a common rejection cause
4. **Last 6 payslips** (tlushei maskoret)
5. **Bank account details** (snif, cheshbon) for benefit deposits
6. **Marriage certificate** if claiming as married
7. **Birth certificates** of dependent children
8. **For resigners claiming justified cause**: supporting documentation (occupational physician's letter, harassment complaint, spouse job offer, marriage certificate, etc.). Note that a regular GP letter is not accepted for health-deterioration grounds, must be a `rofeh tasukati`
9. **For returning Israelis from a treaty country**: foreign social-insurance statement plus form בל/627 (claims foreign months under bilateral SS treaty)

## Bituach Leumi Forms

The online unified form replaces most paper forms, but here are the key forms that may still be requested:

| Form | Hebrew name | When |
|------|-------------|------|
| Main claim (online) | תביעה לדמי אבטלה | Primary application via gov.il portal |
| Form 1514 (employer data) | אישור המעסיק | Employer confirmation of employment details. If the employer refuses to fill it out (common in fired-for-cause disputes), substitute under BL חוזר אבטלה 1342 with: a tatzhir + 3 payslips + bank statements showing wage transfers |
| Form 5040 (bank details) | פרטי חשבון בנק | Bank account for benefit deposit |
| Form 126 (yearly summary) | טופס 126 | Employer's annual tax summary for the claimant. NOT the monthly Form 100 |
| Form 5320 (income supplementation) | בקשה להשלמת הכנסה | When low avtala plus dependents qualifies for hashlamat hachnasa stacking |
| Form בל/627 (foreign social-insurance treaty) | אישור תקופות ביטוח לפי אמנה | Counts foreign work months toward akhshara for returning Israelis |
| Appeal form (irur) | ערעור על החלטת ביטוח לאומי | When appealing a denial or underpayment |

## Sherut HaTaasuka Process

1. **Online registration** at taasuka.gov.il (preferred)
2. **In-person follow-up at the local branch within 14 days** of online registration. The online step is NOT valid without this. Bring original teudat zehut + sefach + all docs
3. **Initial interview** with a caseworker to set up job-seeking plan
4. **Scheduled reporting days** (yemei harshama) assigned, typically every 2 weeks
5. **Job search quota**: must apply to a minimum number of suitable jobs per reporting period
6. **Acceptance of offers**: refusing a "suitable" offer triggers penalty. Suitability test (post-2024 BL clarification): salary within 25% of prior wage, commute under 60 minutes (or remote-eligible), profession within one degree of prior role. After day 60 the test relaxes
7. **Vocational training** (kursim mukarim, maslulei mahalehet): when assigned, attendance is mandatory. Refusing = 90-day wait + 30-day deduction. While in training, avtala continues and an enhanced kiyum stipend may apply

## Appeal Process (Irur)

If the claim is denied or underpaid, there are three escalation steps. The deadlines are different at each level, and confusing them is the most common reason an appeal gets thrown out as untimely.

| Step | Window | Where | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|-------|
| Informal review (va'adat tvi'ot) | 6 months from the decision | Local BL branch in writing | Attach new evidence; non-binding internal review |
| Formal appeal | 12 months from the original decision | Beit Din Ezorit L'Avoda (Regional Labor Court) | Can self-represent for simple cases; lawyer recommended for complex ones |
| Appeal of a Va'adat Ararim ruling specifically | 60 days from the Va'adat Ararim decision | Beit Din Ezorit L'Avoda | This shorter window applies ONLY when escalating from a formal Va'adat Ararim ruling, not from the original BL decision. Very easy to confuse with the 12-month window |

**Free legal aid** is available through Hasneguria HaMishpatit (Public Defender) for low-income claimants, or via Kolzchut online resources for self-representation guidance.

## Reporting Income While Receiving Benefits

If the claimant takes on part-time or temporary work while drawing dmei avtala:

- **Must report immediately** via the Bituach Leumi portal or caseworker
- **Benefit reduction**: roughly 75% of the part-time gross wage is deducted from that month's avtala. Below the BL kotzbat patur (small-earnings exemption), the reduction may not apply, verify thresholds
- **Avtala days used**: partial workdays still count against the max days
- **Failure to report** = fraud = retroactive clawback + possible criminal liability

## Stacking Other BL Benefits

| Benefit | Stack with avtala? |
|---------|-------------------|
| Hashlamat hachnasa (form 5320) | Yes, for low earners with dependents. Typical ₪1,500-2,000/month supplementation |
| Dmei lida | No, blocks avtala for the leda period |
| Kiztavat nechut klalit (75%+) | No, blocks avtala |
| Partial disability (under 75%) | Sometimes, BL evaluates per case |
| Kiztavat zikna | No, applies after age 67 instead |
| Tagmulei miluim | Yes, miluim service days do not consume avtala max-days |
