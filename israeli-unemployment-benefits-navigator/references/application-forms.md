# Dmei Avtala Application Forms and Portals

All official forms, portals, and contact points for Israeli unemployment claims.

## Primary Portals

| Portal | URL | Purpose |
|--------|-----|---------|
| Sherut HaTaasuka (Employment Service) | https://www.taasuka.gov.il | Register as job seeker, attend reporting, job search |
| Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) | https://www.btl.gov.il | File unemployment claim, check status, appeal decisions |
| Government Login (zehut digitalit) | https://www.gov.il | Unified digital ID for all online services |

## Required Documents

Gather these before starting the online application:

1. **Teudat Zehut** (ID card) + sefach (appendix)
2. **Termination letter** (mikhtav piturin) signed by employer, stating the termination date
3. **Form 126** (tofes 126) from the employer for the current year
4. **Last 6 payslips** (tlushei maskoret)
5. **Bank account details** (snif, cheshbon) for benefit deposits
6. **Marriage certificate** if claiming as married
7. **Birth certificates** of dependent children
8. **For resigners claiming justified cause**: supporting documentation (medical letters, harassment complaint, spouse job offer, marriage certificate, etc.)

## Bituach Leumi Forms

The online unified form replaces most paper forms, but here are the key forms that may still be requested:

| Form | Hebrew name | When |
|------|-------------|------|
| Main claim (online) | תביעה לדמי אבטלה | Primary application via gov.il portal |
| Form 1514 (employer data) | אישור המעסיק | Employer confirmation of employment details |
| Form 5040 (bank details) | פרטי חשבון בנק | Bank account for benefit deposit |
| Form 126 (yearly summary) | טופס 126 | Employer's annual tax summary for the claimant |
| Appeal form (irur) | ערעור על החלטת ביטוח לאומי | When appealing a denial or underpayment |

## Sherut HaTaasuka Process

1. **Online registration** at taasuka.gov.il (preferred) OR in person at local branch
2. **Initial interview** with a caseworker to set up job-seeking plan
3. **Scheduled reporting days** (yemei harshama) assigned, typically every 2 weeks
4. **Job search quota**: must apply to a minimum number of suitable jobs per reporting period
5. **Acceptance of offers**: refusing a suitable offer triggers penalty

## Appeal Process (Irur)

If the claim is denied or underpaid:

1. **Informal review**: Ask Bituach Leumi for a reconsideration by writing to the local branch within 60 days of the decision. Include new documentation.
2. **Formal appeal to Labor Court**: File a claim at Beit Din Ezorit L'Avoda (Regional Labor Court) within 12 months of the decision. Can be done with a lawyer or self-represented for simple cases.
3. **Free legal aid**: Available through Hasneguria HaMishpatit (Public Defender) for low-income claimants, or Kolzchut resources for self-representation guidance.

## Expected Timeline

| Milestone | Typical timing |
|-----------|----------------|
| Online registration at Sherut HaTaasuka | Same day |
| Unified claim filing at gov.il | Same day |
| First caseworker meeting | 1-2 weeks |
| First benefit payment | 30-45 days after registration |
| Appeal decision | 3-6 months |

## Reporting Income While Receiving Benefits

If the claimant takes on part-time or temporary work while drawing dmei avtala:

- **Must report immediately** via the Bituach Leumi portal or caseworker
- **Benefit reduction**: roughly 75% of the part-time gross wage is deducted from that month's avtala
- **Avtala days used**: partial workdays still count against the max days
- **Failure to report** = fraud = retroactive clawback + possible criminal liability
