# Vehicle Ownership Transfer Guide (העברת בעלות)

## Before the Sale

### Seller Checklist
- [ ] Ensure vehicle registration (rishayon rechev) is current
- [ ] Clear any outstanding fines or fees on the vehicle
- [ ] Check that the vehicle is not blocked in the system
- [ ] Gather vehicle license document and your ID
- [ ] Agree on price and payment method with buyer

### Buyer Checklist
- [ ] Check vehicle history via gov.il vehicle ownership report
- [ ] **Check for liens at the Registrar of Pledges (רשם המשכונות)** at the Ministry of Justice. This is critical and often overlooked
- [ ] Consider a pre-purchase inspection at an authorized station
- [ ] Verify the seller's identity matches the vehicle registration
- [ ] Arrange insurance (at minimum, mandatory bituach chova) to start from the transfer date
- [ ] Prepare payment and signed sale agreement

## Transfer Methods

### Online (gov.il) -- 235 NIS
**Requirements:**
- Both parties must be Israeli citizens
- Both must have gov.il accounts with digital identification
- Both must be logged in during the process
- Credit card for payment

**Process:**
1. Both parties log into gov.il
2. Seller initiates the transfer
3. Buyer confirms and pays the fee
4. Transfer is recorded immediately

### Post Office -- 250 NIS
**Requirements:**
- Both parties present in person
- Current vehicle license
- IDs of both parties (teudat zehut)
- Cash or credit card for payment

**Process:**
1. Both parties go to any Israel Post branch
2. Present documents to the clerk
3. Pay the transfer fee
4. Receive confirmation

### Licensing Bureau (Misrad Harishui)
**When to use:**
- One or both parties are not Israeli citizens
- Special circumstances (company vehicles, inheritance, etc.)

**Requirements:**
- Current vehicle license
- IDs or passports of both parties
- Power of attorney if one party cannot attend
- Additional documentation as required

## Important Rules

### The 15-Day Deadline
- Transfer must be completed within **15 days** of the sale
- The seller remains legally responsible for the vehicle until transfer is complete
- Any fines or violations during this gap period are the seller's problem
- Delays can cause administrative complications for both parties

### Liens and Encumbrances (עיקולים)
- **The Ministry of Transport system does NOT show all liens**
- The post office may process a transfer even if a lien exists
- Liens attach to the vehicle, not the owner
- If a lien exists, the creditor can seize the vehicle from the new owner
- **Always check with the Registrar of Pledges before buying**

### Insurance
- The buyer must arrange insurance effective from the transfer date
- At minimum, mandatory insurance (bituach chova) is required
- Driving without valid insurance is a criminal offense in Israel
- The seller's insurance does not automatically transfer to the buyer
