# Vehicle Test (טסט) Preparation Checklist

## Before Going to the Test

### Quick Fixes (Handle These First)
- [ ] Check all lights: headlights (low and high), turn signals, brake lights, reverse lights, license plate lights, parking lights
- [ ] Replace any burned-out bulbs
- [ ] Ensure windshield wipers work and are not worn/cracked
- [ ] Check tire tread depth (minimum 1.6mm) and pressure
- [ ] Inspect tires for cracks, bulges, or wrong size
- [ ] Inflate spare tire and ensure it is the correct size
- [ ] Check for oil and water leaks under the vehicle

### Safety Equipment (Required)
- [ ] Reflective safety vest (in accessible location, not in trunk)
- [ ] Warning triangle
- [ ] Basic tool kit
- [ ] Inflated spare tire with jack and wrench
- [ ] Fire extinguisher (recommended but not always required for private vehicles)

### Pre-Test for Older Vehicles
- For vehicles **15+ years old**: get braking system checked at a licensed garage BEFORE the test. Bring the confirmation certificate
- Consider a pre-test inspection at a private garage (200-300 NIS) to catch issues early

## At the Test Station

### What to Bring
- Vehicle registration document (rishayon rechev)
- Valid mandatory insurance certificate (bituach chova)
- For 15+ year old vehicles: brake check confirmation from licensed garage
- Photo ID

### The Test Process
1. Registration and payment at reception
2. Visual exterior inspection
3. Under-vehicle inspection (on a lift)
4. Brake dynamometer test
5. Headlight alignment check
6. Emissions test (tailpipe measurement)
7. Steering system check
8. Results and certificate (if passed)

### If You Fail
- You receive a detailed report listing all defects
- Fix the issues at any garage of your choice
- Return for a retest (partial fee may apply)
- Some stations offer same-day retests for minor fixes

## Common Failure Reasons

### Minor (Easy to Fix)
- Burned-out light bulbs (most common single reason)
- Missing or deflated spare tire
- Missing safety equipment (vest, triangle)
- Worn wiper blades
- Cracked windshield (depends on severity and location)

### Moderate
- Tire issues (wrong size, excessive wear, old age)
- Minor oil leaks
- Worn brake pads
- Headlight misalignment

### Serious (May Need Professional Repair)
- Brake system failures
- Steering system excessive play
- Chassis rust or cracks
- Emissions exceeding limits (catalytic converter issues)
- Suspension damage
