# Israel Land Authority (RMI) System Guide

## Israeli Land System
- ~93% of land is state-owned (Israel Land Authority)
- ~7% privately owned (registered in Tabu)
- Leasehold model: 49-98 year leases
- 2009 reform: Allows conversion to ownership (residential)

## Affordable Housing Program: Dira BeHanacha
The affordable-housing lottery program, historically called "Mechir Lamishtaken"
(מחיר למשתכן), has been rebranded as "Dira BeHanacha" (דירה בהנחה). It is
administered by the Ministry of Construction and Housing (משרד הבינוי והשיכון),
not by RMI directly. The flow is two-step:

1. RMI tenders the land to developers under special conditions (reduced land price in exchange for below-market unit sale prices).
2. The Ministry of Construction and Housing runs eligibility (teudat zakaut), registration, and the random drawing for end buyers at `dira.moch.gov.il`.

Agents helping users "sign up for mechir lamishtaken" should direct them to
the Housing Ministry registration site, not the RMI tender portal.
