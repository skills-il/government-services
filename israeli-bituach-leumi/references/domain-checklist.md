# Domain Checklist: israeli-bituach-leumi

Anchor for Expert Review. Covers National Insurance contributions AND benefits.

## Must cover (core)
- Contribution rates BY PAYER CATEGORY, every row of the official tables, not just the standard case:
  - Employee by age and pension status (18-to-retirement 4.27/12.17; under-18 and old-age-pension recipient 0; 67-70 non-pensioner 3.93/10.03; woman ret-to-67 non-pensioner 3.95/10.24; disability-pension recipient 3.23/5.17; soldier/organ-donor/treaty-resident NI-only) -- btl.gov.il employee rates page.
  - Controlling shareholder (בעל שליטה) variants and naturalized-over-62 variants -- same page.
  - Self-employed by age and pension status (standard 7.7/18; under-18 or pensioner 0.26/0.78 NI, pensioner health = flat 237/mo; past-retirement non-pensioner 6.92/15.79) -- btl.gov.il self-employed rates page.
  - Early-pension recipients (4.25/11.96, withheld by the pension payer) -- btl.gov.il early-pension rates page.
  - Household-help employers (6.05% employer + 2.8% worker = 8.85%; 3.6% for minor/pensioner worker).
  - Non-working residents and non-work (passive) income: 266 minimum, 3,442 exemption, 12.09%/12.17%.
- The reduced-collection step (מדרגת גביה מופחתת) is a STATUTORY amount (base 7,522, CPI-indexed 2026-2028), NOT 60% of the average wage. Contributions ceiling 51,910 vs wage-replacing-benefit ceiling 52,570 are DIFFERENT numbers.
- Unpaid leave (chalat): employer pays the first 2 months (deductible from wage), employee pays the 266 minimum from month 3 -- btl.gov.il Employee_in_vacation page.
- Contribution EXEMPTIONS (override the rate tables): non-working spouse / akeret bayit = 0 (Form 673); old-age pension recipients / retirement age = NI-portion exempt, 688 NIS non-work-income floor; disability 75%+/100% permanent = exempt.
- Coordination: passive-income exemption does NOT stack on work income; te'um dmei bituach for multiple employers / salary plus early pension.
- The 13 main benefit programs with 2026 amounts and qualifying periods -- references/benefit-programs.md.
- INCOME TESTS must be stated as graduated bands, not cliffs:
  - Old-age (retirement age to 70), work income: full below 10,113 (no spouse) / 13,484 (with spouse); partial to 14,402 / 20,082; none above. Pension income not counted.
  - Long-term care (from 1.4.2026): single full to 13,769, 50%-reduced to 20,654, none above; couple full to 20,654, 50%-reduced to 30,980, none above.
- Update cadence: amounts are reissued on 1 JANUARY (not twice a year). Individual tables can be reissued mid-year (siyud, 1.4.2026).
- Second-tier programs with their own entitlement: alimony (5400), personal-accident / dmei te'una (2201), vocational rehabilitation (270), burial grant (460, exceptional cases), death grant (416), study grant, discharged-soldier preferred-work grant, volunteer injury.
- Status layer: residency (toshavut) as the gateway, contribution gaps breaking the qualifying period, employer non-payment (BTL pays and pursues the employer), equity grant (form 20), BTL debt enforcement, overpayment clawback, self-service certificates.
- Claim forms per benefit (480, 1500, 355, 7801, 2201, etc.) -- references/forms.md.
- Scope disclaimers: ma'anak avoda (EITC) is Tax Authority, not BTL; sick pay is employer-paid, not BTL.


## Must cover (benefits side) -- EVERY amount below must be enumerated along EVERY dimension it varies by

The recurring failure on this skill is quoting a BANDED amount as a flat figure. For each benefit, enumerate the dimension(s) and check each row against the source table.

- **Old-age retirement age varies by SEX and, for women, by BIRTH YEAR.** Men 67. Women 62 (up to birth-year 1959), 62y4m (1960), 62y8m (1961), 63 (1962), 63y3m (1963), 63y6m (1964), 63y9m (1965), 64 (1966), 64y3m (1967), 64y6m (1968), 64y9m (1969), **65 only from 1970**. Never say "65 for women born after 1960" -- btl.gov.il .../gilMezake/Pages/gilPrisha.aspx
- **Old-age pension amount varies by family composition** (single 1,838 / single 80+ 1,941 / couple 2,762 / couple 80+ 2,865 / plus child supplements 2,419, 3,343, 3,000, 3,924).
- **Old-age income test (retirement age to 70) is a GRADUATED band**, not a cliff: full below 10,113 (no spouse) / 13,484 (spouse); partial to 14,402 / 20,082. Pension income is NOT counted.
- **Unemployment duration varies by AGE and by DEPENDANTS, where "dependants" means 3 OR MORE family members**: up to 25: 50 (138); 25-28: 67 (138); 28-35: 100 (138); 35-45: 138 (175); 45+: 175 unconditional. **Women 57-67 born 1960+: 300 days over 18 months.** Discharged soldiers: 70 days in year one -- kolzchut "מספר הימים המירבי"; btl.gov.il/benefits/Unemployment/Pages/zecoyot-nasim.aspx
- **Unemployment replacement rate varies by AGE**: capped at 60% of wage under 28, 80% from 28, degressive above ~5,000/month. BTL publishes TWO tables -- btl.gov.il/benefits/Unemployment/Pages/sum.aspx. Daily caps 550.76 (days 1-125) and 367.17 (from day 126), both CAPS not floors.
- **Income support varies by AGE and by FAMILY COMPOSITION** (all 6 compositions x 2 age bands; ages 20-24 split by registering vs exempt; +825 for women born 1960-1964). Never quote the single rate to a parent.
- **Survivors pension varies by AGE and CHILDREN** (40-50 childless 1,381; 50+ 1,838; 80+ 1,941; +1 child 2,700; +2 children 3,562; +862 each extra). **A widow/widower under 40 with no children gets a one-time survivors GRANT worth 36 monthly pensions**, not nothing (a widower married to an akeret bayit is excluded). The 12-month filing window is a RETROACTIVITY cap, not a forfeiture.
- **Long-term care (siyud): income test AND cash amounts were BOTH reissued effective 1.4.2026.** Cash varies by level AND by foreign vs Israeli caregiver (L1 1,705/1,705 ... L6 6,448/7,440). Income test: single full to 13,769, 50%-reduced to 20,654; couple full to 20,654, 50%-reduced to 30,980.
- **General-disability FAMILY SUPPLEMENTS are banded BY DEGREE** (spouse 1,518 / 1,123 / 987 / 911; each child 1,214 / 898 / 789 / 728 at 75-100 / 74 / 65 / 60%), and the spouse supplement is income-conditional (spouse gross <= 7,848/mo, no other benefit). Never quote the full-degree supplement to a partial-degree claimant.
- **Disabled child (yeled nacheh) has FIVE levels** (1,943 / 3,820 / 4,501 / 7,181 / 9,126). 3,820 is the 100% level, NOT the ceiling. Ventilated +10,774; two caregivers +7,182; a family with 2+ disabled children gets every child's rate raised by 50%; institutionalised 673 / 1,347.
- **The 300-day unemployment cohort has TWO sub-bands**: women 57-60 also have the daily benefit capped at 201.03 from day 176 (and a training-refusal penalty of 90 days + 30 days off, leaving 270); women 60-67 do not. A duration rule and its payment rule travel together.
- **Seniority supplement (תוספת ותק) is 2% of the BASIC pension for EVERY full insured year, counted from year ONE** (BTL's worked example: 15 years = 30%), capped at 50% (reached at 25 years). It does NOT start at year 11. The spouse (924) and child (581) increments are separate תוספות ADDED ON TOP of the seniority-inflated basic rate, not multiplied by it. This rule survived six review rounds while wrong, because the wrong rule and the right cap were written next to each other -- always check a rule against its own cap for arithmetic consistency.
- **Survivors amounts are BASE amounts:** a seniority supplement (and, for orphans, a subsistence supplement) is paid on top, so every survivors figure is a floor.
- **Old-age deferral (+5%/yr) COMPOUNDS on the seniority-inflated pension** and is capped at (70 - retirement age), so it is 3 years max for a man and up to 8 for a woman retiring at 62. Child supplement 581 each, max 2.
- **Birth grant multiple births are banded**: twins 10,514, triplets 15,771, +5,257 per additional baby. A boolean --twins flag cannot model this.
- **Special services (SHRM) has FOUR tiers** (50/112/188/235 = 1,943/4,501/7,181/9,126). The 10,774 supplement is for a VENTILATED recipient at 188%+ and is not payable with the 235% rate. There is no 100% or 150% tier.
- Every figure must agree across SKILL.md, SKILL_HE.md, references/*, AND scripts/calculate_benefits.py. The script is the artifact the agent executes: a stale number there ships next to the corrected prose.

## Should cover (advanced)
- New-immigrant (oleh) contribution reductions in the early residency window.
- Residents working abroad for a foreign employer (bilateral SS treaty coordination, forms 627/628).

## Out of scope (explicit)
- Under-18 separate liability (covered via household, not separately liable).
- Private insurance / kupat cholim membership questions (different domain).
- Detailed appeal litigation procedure (the skill points to the labor court; full procedure is legal-tech scope).
- Deep residency-determination rules, BTL enforcement/lien procedure, and treaty totalization mechanics: named and routed in SKILL.md "Status, Obligations, and Debt", but the full rule-set is a separate skill's scope.
- Ringworm / polio / blood-transfusion victim compensation laws, Prisoners of Zion, Righteous Among the Nations: narrow named populations, deliberately not covered.
