# Bituach Leumi Contribution Rates (2026)

Full per-category contribution tables. SKILL.md carries the summary; this file carries every row, the NI/health split, the controlling-shareholder and naturalized-over-62 variants, and the exemptions.

All figures effective 01.01.2026 unless noted.

## Anchors

| Anchor | Value |
|---|---|
| Reduced-collection step (מדרגת גביה מופחתת) | 7,703 NIS/month |
| Maximum income for contributions | 51,910 NIS/month (quarterly 155,730) |
| Basic amount (הסכום הבסיסי) | 10,514 NIS/month |
| Ceiling for wage-replacing benefits | 52,570 NIS/month (5 x basic amount) |
| Average wage, section 2 (benefits) | 13,769 NIS/month |
| Average wage, section 1 | 13,566 NIS/month |

**The 7,703 step is a statutory amount, NOT 60% of the average wage.** The law fixes a base of 7,522 NIS, updated each 1 January by the CPI for 2026 to 2028 and by the average wage from 2029. Deriving it as 0.6 x 13,769 gives 8,261, which is wrong (7,703 is 56% of the average wage). Source: BTL employer circular 1522.

## Employees (full table, by category)

|---|---|---|---|---|
| Aged 18 to retirement age (standard) | 1.04% NI + 3.23% health = **4.27%** | 7.0% + 5.17% = **12.17%** | 4.51% | 7.60% |
| Controlling shareholder, closely-held company (בעל שליטה) | **4.25%** | **11.96%** | 4.46% | 7.38% |
| Under 18 | **0%** | **0%** | 0.61% | 2.12% |
| Receiving old-age pension, any age | **0%** | **0%** | 0.61% | 2.12% |
| Reached retirement age, under 70, NOT receiving old-age pension | 0.61% + 3.23% = **3.93%** | 4.86% + 5.17% = **10.03%** | 4.13% | 6.96% |
| Woman between her retirement age and 67, NOT receiving old-age pension | **3.95%** | **10.24%** | 4.17% | 7.12% |
| Receiving work-injury or general-disability pension (with annual BTL confirmation) | 0% NI + 3.23% health = **3.23%** | 0% + 5.17% = **5.17%** | 0.61% | 2.12% |
| Soldier in regular service, organ donor, treaty-country foreign resident | 1.04% NI only (health exempt) | 7.0% NI only | 4.51% | 7.60% |

The employer share changes too, and it is NOT always 4.51% / 7.60%: for a minor, an old-age-pension recipient, or a disability-pension recipient the employer pays a reduced 0.61% / 2.12%, and for an employee aged 67-70 (or a woman between her retirement age and 67) who is not drawing a pension the employer pays 4.13% / 6.96% (4.17% / 7.12% respectively). Read the employer columns above, do not assume the standard rate.

## Self-employed

| Category | Up to 7,703 | 7,703 to 51,910 |
|---|---|---|
| Aged 18 to retirement age (standard) | 4.47% NI + 3.23% health = **7.7%** | 12.83% + 5.17% = **18.0%** |
| Under 18 | **0.26%** NI only (health exempt) | **0.78%** NI only |
| Receiving old-age pension (any age) | **0.26%** NI, plus health at a flat **237 NIS/month** off the pension | **0.78%** NI |
| Reached retirement age, NOT receiving old-age pension | **6.92%** | **15.79%** |

On reaching retirement age a self-employed person stops paying for the disability and work-accident branches, which is why the rate drops. Income under 3,442 NIS/month is charged on the minimum-income basis. Self-employed pay both the employee and employer shares and cannot claim unemployment. Source: `btl.gov.il/Insurance/Rates/Pages/לעצמאים.aspx`.

## Household-help employers (ma'asik oved meshek bayit)

A private individual employing a domestic worker (cleaner, nanny, caregiver, gardener) must register with BTL and pay quarterly on the worker's wage, not through a monthly payslip system.

| Worker | Employer pays | Worker's share | Total |
|---|---|---|---|
| Aged 18 to retirement age | **6.05%** NI (0% health on the employer side) | **2.8%** (1.8% NI + 1% health) | **8.85%** |
| Under 18 or receiving an old-age pension | **3.6%** | -- | 3.6% |

Source: `btl.gov.il/Insurance/Rates/Pages/עובד משק בית.aspx`.

## Unpaid leave (chalat)

- First 2 months: the **employer** pays, and may deduct what it paid from the employee's wage.
- From the 3rd month: a person not working as employee or self-employed pays the non-worker minimum of **266 NIS/month** themselves. If they draw unemployment, BTL deducts it from the benefit.
- Gaps here break the qualifying period (תקופת אכשרה) for later claims. Source: `btl.gov.il/Insurance/National Insurance/type_list/Employee_in_vacation/Pages/schum.aspx`.

## Multiple employers and coordination (te'um dmei bituach)

Each employer applies the reduced bracket to the salary IT pays, so an employee with two employers (or a salary plus an early pension) gets the reduced bracket more than once and is over-deducted. The secondary employer (and an early-pension payer) must deduct at the FULL rate from the first shekel UNLESS the employee files a coordination request (te'um dmei bituach). File te'um through the secondary employer, or claim a refund from BTL after year-end. This is one of the most common over-payments for people with a second job or a gig plus a salary.

## Non-working residents and non-work (passive) income

A working-age resident (18 to retirement age) who is NOT salaried, NOT self-employed, and NOT an exempt non-working spouse owes contributions on their own account. Read the exemptions below before applying this table, they change the answer for several common cases.

| Situation (own account, no work income) | Monthly charge |
|---|---|
| Not working, no income | **Minimum 266 NIS/month** (143 NI + 123 health) |
| Non-work income up to 3,442 NIS/month | Exempt from the rate, but the 266 minimum still applies |
| Non-work income 3,442 to 7,703 (reduced tier) | 6.92% NI + 5.17% health = **12.09%** on the amount above 3,442 |
| Non-work income 7,703 to 51,910 (full tier) | 7.0% + 5.17% = **12.17%** |

"Non-work income" (הכנסה שלא מעבודה) means passive income such as rent, dividends, and interest. The first 3,442 NIS/month is exempt; rates apply only above it. The 266 minimum is a floor. BTL sends a quarterly advance-payment book (pinkas mikdamot) to non-employees.

### Exemptions and exceptions (these OVERRIDE the table above)

| Who | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Non-working spouse (עקרת בית, or a non-working partner of an insured resident, including common-law and same-sex marriage) | **Fully exempt, pays 0.** Granted automatically in most cases; otherwise reported via Form 673 or the Form 6101 multi-year declaration. Lumping this person under the 266 minimum is the single most common mistake. |
| Old-age pension recipient / past retirement age | Special treatment, NOT the flat table. For benefit recipients, non-work income up to **688 NIS/month** is exempt, and the NI portion generally does not apply to a pensioner's income (health may still apply). Verify the exact split with BTL before quoting a number. |
| Person with BOTH work income and passive income | The 3,442 exemption does NOT stack on top of work income. Work income consumes the exemption, so passive income is charged separately (often from the first shekel for higher earners). This is a coordination calculation. |
| New immigrant (oleh) in the early residency window, full-time student, or resident working abroad for a foreign employer | Special rules (oleh reductions, bilateral social-security treaty coordination). Treat as a separate case, not the base table. |

- Under-18s are not separately liable; coverage is through the household.
- The 266 minimum and the 3,442 / 688 thresholds are average-wage-indexed and update on 1 January.
- Sources: `btl.gov.il/Insurance/National Insurance/Pages/מי פטור מתשלום דמי ביטוח לאומי.aspx`, `btl.gov.il/Insurance/National Insurance/type_list/NotWorking/Pages/ScomNotWorking.aspx`, `btl.gov.il/Insurance/Rates`, kolzchut "פטור מתשלום דמי ביטוח לאומי".

## Amendment 252

The 2026 rates were set by Amendment 252 to the National Insurance Law (חוק הביטוח הלאומי (תיקון מס' 252 והוראת שעה), התשפ"ה-2025), enacted 2025-01-14. Rates are CPI-indexed for 2026 through 2028 and switch to wage indexation from 2029.
