# Israeli Miluim Manager

## Instructions

### Step 1: Reserve Duty Basics

Israeli reserve duty (miluim, מילואים) is compulsory military service for citizens who have completed their regular service (sherut seder, שירות סדיר). Understanding the fundamentals is the first step to managing obligations and rights.

| Term | Hebrew | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Miluim | מילואים | Reserve duty service |
| Tsav 8 | צו 8 | Emergency call-up order |
| Tsav Kriah | צו קריאה | Routine call-up notice |
| Keva | קבע | Career military (not miluim) |
| Mashak | משק | Unit administrative coordinator |
| Gisheet | גישית | Personal IDF portal |

**Who serves in miluim (current temporary order, valid through June 30, 2027):**
- Men (non-officers) up to age 41, officers up to age 46
- Special professions (drivers, pilots, physicians) up to age 49-50
- Women in specific roles until age 38 (combat roles may serve until equivalent male age)
- Single women without children serve until age 24 (age 34 in special professions)
- Exemptions exist for medical reasons, religious study (hesder/yeshiva arrangements), and single parents under certain conditions

**Call-up process:**
1. Receive Tsav Kriah (routine) or Tsav 8 (emergency) via mail, phone, or IDF systems
2. Report to designated base on the specified date
3. Service length varies: routine training (1-4 weeks/year) or extended during operations
4. Upon release, receive Form 3010 (tofes 3010, טופס 3010) confirming service dates

### Step 2: Employment Protections

Israeli law provides strong employment protections for reservists under the Reserve Duty Compensation Law (Chok Tagmulei Miluim, חוק תגמולי מילואים) and the Military Service Law.

| Protection | Details (2026) |
|------------|----------------|
| Job preservation | Employer must hold the reservist's position during service |
| Dismissal ban (post-service, under 60 days served) | 30 days after return from service |
| Dismissal ban (post-service, 60+ days served) | 60 days after return (permanent law since 2026) |
| Dismissal ban (pre-service) | Cannot fire employee due to upcoming miluim call-up |
| Seniority | Service period counts toward seniority and tenure |
| Pension contributions | Employer must continue pension deposits during service |
| Work conditions | Must return to same role, same terms, same pay |
| 20% social contribution compensation | State compensates private employers 20% of salary for social contributions (permanent since 2026) |

**Key changes for 2026:**
- The 60-day post-service protection that was a wartime temporary order has been **made permanent law** for reservists serving 60+ days. Reservists serving fewer than 60 days receive the standard 30-day protection.
- The 20% employer compensation for social contributions (pension, national insurance) during reserve service was **made permanent** from January 2026. The state reimburses private employers 20% of the reserve-serving employee's salary for social contributions.
- The wartime supplementary employer compensation (for hiring temp replacements) has expired.

**Filing a complaint:**
If an employer violates these protections, the reservist can file a complaint with the Labor Court (Beit Din LaAvoda, בית דין לעבודה). Filing fee is 1% of the claim amount, minimum NIS 168. Many common labor claims (wages, overtime, holiday pay, vacation pay) are exempt from filing fees.

### Step 3: Compensation Through Bituach Leumi

Bituach Leumi (ביטוח לאומי, National Insurance Institute) compensates reservists for lost income during service through reserve duty compensation (tagmulei miluim, תגמולי מילואים).

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Compensation basis | Average income from 3 months before service |
| Maximum daily rate | Updated annually by Bituach Leumi |
| Payment timing | Employer advances salary, then claims reimbursement |
| Self-employed | File directly with Bituach Leumi |
| Minimum daily compensation | NIS 328.76/day (NIS 9,863/month equivalent, 2026 rate) |

**How to file for compensation (employees):**
1. Employer submits claim via Bituach Leumi employer portal
2. Attach Form 3010 confirming service dates
3. Bituach Leumi reimburses the employer within 30-60 days
4. Employee receives regular salary as usual

**How to file for compensation (self-employed):**
1. Log in to Bituach Leumi personal portal
2. Submit reserve duty compensation claim (tvia letagmulei miluim, תביעה לתגמולי מילואים)
3. Upload Form 3010 and income documentation
4. Payment deposited directly to bank account

**Minimum compensation for low earners:**
Reservists earning below the minimum compensation floor (NIS 9,863/month in 2026) receive a top-up from Bituach Leumi to reach that floor during service months. This applies automatically. For reservists with very low or no income, the gap can be approximately NIS 3,000/month, but the exact amount depends on the individual's prior reported income.

### Step 4: 2026 Tax Benefits for Combat Reservists

Amendment 283 to the Income Tax Ordinance (Section 39B), passed November 19, 2025, introduced a graduated tax credit system for **combat reservists** (lochamim) based on days served. These apply to tax years 2026-2027 and are managed through the Israel Tax Authority (Rashut HaMisim, רשות המסים).

**Important:** These tax credits apply only to **combat service days** (yamei lochem). Non-combat reserve service qualifies for the standard reservist credit of 1 credit point (NIS 2,904/year).

| Combat Service Days Per Year | Credit Points | Annual Credit Value (NIS) |
|------------------------------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Under 30 days | Standard (1.0) | 2,904 |
| 30-39 days | 0.50 | 1,452 |
| 40-49 days | 0.75 | 2,178 |
| 50-54 days | 1.00 | 2,904 |
| 55-59 days | 1.25 | 3,630 |
| 60-64 days | 1.50 | 4,356 |
| 65-69 days | 1.75 | 5,082 |
| 70-74 days | 2.00 | 5,808 |
| 75-79 days | 2.25 | 6,534 |
| 80-84 days | 2.50 | 7,260 |
| 85-89 days | 2.75 | 7,986 |
| 90-94 days | 3.00 | 8,712 |
| 95-99 days | 3.25 | 9,438 |
| 100-104 days | 3.50 | 10,164 |
| 105-109 days | 3.75 | 10,890 |
| 110+ days | 4.00 | 11,616 |

One credit point (nekudat zikui) = NIS 242/month = NIS 2,904/year (2026 value, frozen through 2027).

**How to claim tax credits:**
1. Obtain service confirmation from IDF (ishur sherut miluim, אישור שירות מילואים)
2. Submit annual tax credit form (tofes 101, טופס 101) to employer, or file directly with Rashut HaMisim
3. Credits applied to monthly payroll deductions or as annual refund

### Step 5: Employer Obligations

Employers bear specific responsibilities toward reservist employees. Several wartime temporary measures were made permanent in 2026.

| Obligation | Status (2026) |
|------------|---------------|
| Hold position during service | Active (permanent law) |
| Continue pension contributions | Active (permanent law) |
| Advance salary during service | Active (permanent law) |
| 20% compensation for social contributions | Active (made permanent from January 2026, private employers only) |
| 30-day post-service dismissal ban | Active (permanent law, for service under 60 days) |
| 60-day post-service dismissal ban | Active (permanent law, for service of 60+ days) |
| Supplementary employer compensation (temp replacements) | Expired |

**Active employer obligations:**
- Advance the reservist's regular salary during service and claim reimbursement from Bituach Leumi
- Continue all social contributions (pension, keren hishtalmut, health insurance) during service, with 20% of salary reimbursed by the state
- Restore the employee to the same position and terms upon return
- Maintain seniority accrual during the service period

### Step 6: Freelancer and Self-Employed Reservist Rights

Self-employed reservists (atzmai, עצמאי) and freelancers (frilanser, פרילנסר) have specific entitlements.

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Direct compensation | File directly with Bituach Leumi (no employer intermediary) |
| Income basis | Average of 3 months prior to service |
| Business continuity | No law requires clients to hold contracts, but anti-discrimination applies |
| Tax credits | Same combat credit tiers as employees (if serving in combat role) |
| Minimum compensation | NIS 9,863/month floor (2026), topped up by Bituach Leumi if income is below this |

**Steps for self-employed reservists:**
1. Document all client contracts and expected income before service
2. Notify clients of upcoming absence
3. Upon release, file compensation claim with Bituach Leumi using Form 3010
4. Claim tax credits through annual tax filing with Rashut HaMisim
5. If income dropped significantly, consult a tax advisor about additional deductions

### Step 7: Vacation Days and Spouse Protections

Several temporary provisions enacted during wartime operations have been resolved for 2026, some made permanent and some expired.

| Provision | Status (2026) |
|-----------|---------------|
| Accumulated vacation days from wartime order | Must be used by end of 2027 |
| Broad spouse unpaid leave entitlement | Expired (wartime temporary order ended Dec 31, 2025) |
| Spouse 1-hour paid absence per day | Active (permanent law, during reservist's service of 5+ consecutive days) |
| Spouse 14-day dismissal protection | Active (permanent law, after reservist's service ends) |
| Extended vacation carry-over | Subject to standard labor law limits |

**Vacation day rules:**
- Reservists who accumulated vacation days during wartime orders must use them by the end of 2027
- Standard labor law vacation accrual resumes: 12-16 days/year depending on tenure
- Employers cannot force reservists to use vacation days during miluim service
- Sick days continue to accrue during reserve service

**Spouse protections (permanent from 2026):**
- Spouses of reservists serving 5+ consecutive days are entitled to 1 hour of paid absence per day during the service period
- Employers cannot dismiss the spouse of a reservist within 14 days after the reservist's return from service

### Step 8: Families of Reservists

Family members of reservists have access to support services and certain rights.

| Benefit | Eligible Family Members |
|---------|------------------------|
| Emergency hotline | Spouse, parents, children |
| Municipal support services | Household members |
| School/daycare priority | Children of active reservists |
| Psychological support | All immediate family |
| Financial emergency assistance | Spouse, dependents |

**Key family resources:**
- IDF Family Support Center (Merkaz Tmicha LeMishpachot, מרכז תמיכה למשפחות): 24/7 hotline
- Municipal welfare departments offer targeted assistance during extended service
- NGOs such as Libi Fund (Keren Libi, קרן ליבי) and IDFWO provide grants and support
- Schools may offer accommodations for children of reservists during extended call-ups

## Gotchas
- Israeli reserve duty (miluim) compensation is paid by Bituach Leumi, not by the employer or the IDF directly. Agents may direct users to the wrong organization for payment claims.
- The Amendment 283 tax credit tiers apply only to **combat service days** (yamei lochem). Non-combat reserve service qualifies for a standard 1-point credit. Agents may apply combat credit tiers to non-combat reservists.
- The number of reserve duty days per year is capped based on age and unit type. Agents may not know the current limits or may apply outdated limits from previous security situations.
- Reserve duty documentation (Form 3010) must be retained for Bituach Leumi claims. Agents may not mention the importance of keeping the original order documents.
- Self-employed reservists receive compensation based on their reported income to Bituach Leumi, which may differ from actual income. Agents may calculate compensation based on current month revenue instead of the Bituach Leumi-reported income.
- Dismissal protection duration depends on length of service: 30 days for under 60 days served, 60 days for 60+ days served. Agents may apply a flat 30-day ban to all reservists regardless of service length.

## Troubleshooting

### Error: "Employer refuses to advance salary during miluim"
Cause: Employer may be unaware of legal obligation or facing cash flow issues.
Solution:
1. Cite the Reserve Duty Compensation Law requiring salary advancement
2. Remind employer that Bituach Leumi reimburses the full amount, plus 20% for social contributions
3. If employer still refuses, file complaint with the Labor Court. Filing fee is 1% of claim, minimum NIS 168 (many common labor claims are exempt from fees)

### Error: "Bituach Leumi rejected compensation claim"
Cause: Missing or incorrect Form 3010, income documentation gaps, or filing deadline exceeded.
Solution:
1. Verify Form 3010 dates match actual service period
2. Ensure income documentation covers the 3 months before service
3. If deadline passed, file an appeal (erur, ערעור) with Bituach Leumi within 6 months
4. Contact Bituach Leumi service center for specific rejection reason

### Error: "Employer terminated reservist within protection period"
Cause: Employer violated post-service dismissal protection (either unaware or deliberate).
Solution:
1. Document termination date and service return date
2. Determine applicable protection period: 30 days (under 60 days served) or 60 days (60+ days served)
3. Send formal letter citing the applicable post-service protection period
4. File complaint with Labor Court; claim reinstatement and compensation
5. Filing fee is 1% of claim, minimum NIS 168 (many common labor claims are exempt from fees)
