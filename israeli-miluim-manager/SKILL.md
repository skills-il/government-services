# Israeli Miluim Manager

## Instructions

### Step 1: Reserve Duty Basics

Israeli reserve duty (miluim, מילואים) is compulsory military service for citizens who have completed their regular service (sherut seder, שירות סדיר). Understanding the fundamentals is the first step to managing obligations and rights.

| Term | Hebrew | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Miluim | מילואים | Reserve duty service |
| Tsav 8 | צו 8 | Emergency call-up order |
| Tsav Kriah | צו קריאה | Routine call-up notice |
| Keva | קבע | Career military (not miluim) |
| Mashak | משק | Unit administrative coordinator |
| Gisheet | גישית | Personal IDF portal |

**Who serves in miluim:**
- Men up to age 40 (combat roles) or 45 (non-combat roles), extended in certain units
- Women in specific roles until age 38
- Exemptions exist for medical reasons, religious study (hesder/yeshiva arrangements), and single parents under certain conditions

**Call-up process:**
1. Receive Tsav Kriah (routine) or Tsav 8 (emergency) via mail, phone, or IDF systems
2. Report to designated base on the specified date
3. Service length varies: routine training (1-4 weeks/year) or extended during operations
4. Upon release, receive Form 3010 (tofes 3010, טופס 3010) confirming service dates

### Step 2: Employment Protections

Israeli law provides strong employment protections for reservists under the Reserve Duty Compensation Law (Chok Tagmulei Miluim, חוק תגמולי מילואים) and the Military Service Law.

| Protection | Details (2026) |
|------------|----------------|
| Job preservation | Employer must hold the reservist's position during service |
| Dismissal ban (post-service) | 30 days after return (was 60 days during wartime, reverted in 2026) |
| Dismissal ban (pre-service) | Cannot fire employee due to upcoming miluim call-up |
| Seniority | Service period counts toward seniority and tenure |
| Pension contributions | Employer must continue pension deposits during service |
| Work conditions | Must return to same role, same terms, same pay |

**Key changes for 2026:**
- The 60-day post-service protection period that was in effect during wartime has reverted to the standard 30 days
- The 20% employer compensation for social contributions expired in January 2026
- Employers can no longer claim the wartime supplementary compensation

**Filing a complaint:**
If an employer violates these protections, the reservist can file a complaint with the Labor Court (Beit Din LaAvoda, בית דין לעבודה). Filing fee is 1% of the claim amount, with a minimum of NIS 50.

### Step 3: Compensation Through Bituach Leumi

Bituach Leumi (ביטוח לאומי, National Insurance Institute) compensates reservists for lost income during service through reserve duty compensation (tagmulei miluim, תגמולי מילואים).

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Compensation basis | Average income from 3 months before service |
| Maximum daily rate | Updated annually by Bituach Leumi |
| Payment timing | Employer advances salary, then claims reimbursement |
| Self-employed | File directly with Bituach Leumi |
| Minimum payment | At least minimum wage equivalent for service days |

**How to file for compensation (employees):**
1. Employer submits claim via Bituach Leumi employer portal
2. Attach Form 3010 confirming service dates
3. Bituach Leumi reimburses the employer within 30-60 days
4. Employee receives regular salary as usual

**How to file for compensation (self-employed):**
1. Log in to Bituach Leumi personal portal
2. Submit reserve duty compensation claim (tvia letagmulei miluim, תביעה לתגמולי מילואים)
3. Upload Form 3010 and income documentation
4. Payment deposited directly to bank account

### Step 4: 2026 Tax Benefits

The 2026 tax year introduced updated credit tiers for reservists based on days served. These are managed through the Israel Tax Authority (Rashut HaMisim, רשות המסים).

| Days of Service Per Year | Tax Credit (2026) |
|--------------------------|-------------------|
| 10-30 days | Standard reservist credit |
| 31-60 days | Enhanced credit tier |
| 61-110 days | Higher credit tier |
| 110+ days | Maximum credit (largest tier, new for 2026) |

**Monthly supplement:**
- Reservists with income below the tax threshold (up to NIS 9,800/month) receive a monthly supplement of approximately NIS 3,000
- This is designed for reservists whose service disrupts lower-income employment
- Applied automatically through Bituach Leumi for eligible reservists

**How to claim tax credits:**
1. Obtain service confirmation from IDF (ishur sherut miluim, אישור שירות מילואים)
2. Submit annual tax credit form (tofes 101, טופס 101) to employer, or file directly with Rashut HaMisim
3. Credits applied to monthly payroll deductions or as annual refund

### Step 5: Employer Obligations and Expired Wartime Measures

Employers bear specific responsibilities toward reservist employees. Several wartime measures have expired as of 2026.

| Obligation | Status (2026) |
|------------|---------------|
| Hold position during service | Active (permanent law) |
| Continue pension contributions | Active (permanent law) |
| Advance salary during service | Active (permanent law) |
| 20% compensation for social contributions | Expired (January 2026) |
| Extended 60-day post-service protection | Expired (reverted to 30 days) |
| Supplementary employer compensation | Expired |

**Active employer obligations:**
- Advance the reservist's regular salary during service and claim reimbursement from Bituach Leumi
- Continue all social contributions (pension, keren hishtalmut, health insurance) during service
- Restore the employee to the same position and terms upon return
- Maintain seniority accrual during the service period

### Step 6: Freelancer and Self-Employed Reservist Rights

Self-employed reservists (atzmai, עצמאי) and freelancers (frilanser, פרילנסר) have specific entitlements.

| Right | Details |
|-------|---------|
| Direct compensation | File directly with Bituach Leumi (no employer intermediary) |
| Income basis | Average of 3 months prior to service |
| Business continuity | No law requires clients to hold contracts, but anti-discrimination applies |
| Tax credits | Same credit tiers as employees |
| Monthly supplement | Eligible if below NIS 9,800/month threshold |

**Steps for self-employed reservists:**
1. Document all client contracts and expected income before service
2. Notify clients of upcoming absence
3. Upon release, file compensation claim with Bituach Leumi using Form 3010
4. Claim tax credits through annual tax filing with Rashut HaMisim
5. If income dropped significantly, consult a tax advisor about additional deductions

### Step 7: Vacation Days and Expired Temporary Provisions

Several temporary provisions enacted during wartime operations have specific expiration dates.

| Provision | Status (2026) |
|-----------|---------------|
| Accumulated vacation days from wartime order | Must be used by end of 2027 |
| Spouse unpaid leave entitlement | NOT in effect for 2026 |
| Extended vacation carry-over | Subject to standard labor law limits |

**Vacation day rules:**
- Reservists who accumulated vacation days during wartime orders must use them by the end of 2027
- Standard labor law vacation accrual resumes: 12-16 days/year depending on tenure
- Employers cannot force reservists to use vacation days during miluim service
- Sick days continue to accrue during reserve service

### Step 8: Families of Reservists

Family members of reservists have access to support services and certain rights.

| Benefit | Eligible Family Members |
|---------|------------------------|
| Emergency hotline | Spouse, parents, children |
| Municipal support services | Household members |
| School/daycare priority | Children of active reservists |
| Psychological support | All immediate family |
| Financial emergency assistance | Spouse, dependents |

**Key family resources:**
- IDF Family Support Center (Merkaz Tmicha LeMishpachot, מרכז תמיכה למשפחות): 24/7 hotline
- Municipal welfare departments offer targeted assistance during extended service
- NGOs such as Libi Fund (Keren Libi, קרן ליבי) and IDFWO provide grants and support
- Schools may offer accommodations for children of reservists during extended call-ups

## Troubleshooting

### Error: "Employer refuses to advance salary during miluim"
Cause: Employer may be unaware of legal obligation or facing cash flow issues.
Solution:
1. Cite the Reserve Duty Compensation Law requiring salary advancement
2. Remind employer that Bituach Leumi reimburses the full amount
3. If employer still refuses, file complaint with the Labor Court (minimum NIS 50 filing fee)

### Error: "Bituach Leumi rejected compensation claim"
Cause: Missing or incorrect Form 3010, income documentation gaps, or filing deadline exceeded.
Solution:
1. Verify Form 3010 dates match actual service period
2. Ensure income documentation covers the 3 months before service
3. If deadline passed, file an appeal (erur, ערעור) with Bituach Leumi within 6 months
4. Contact Bituach Leumi service center for specific rejection reason

### Error: "Employer terminated reservist within 30 days of return"
Cause: Employer violated post-service dismissal protection (either unaware or deliberate).
Solution:
1. Document termination date and service return date
2. Send formal letter citing the 30-day post-service protection
3. File complaint with Labor Court; claim reinstatement and compensation
4. Filing fee is 1% of claim, minimum NIS 50
