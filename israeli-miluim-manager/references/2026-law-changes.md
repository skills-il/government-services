# 2026 Reserve Duty Law Changes

A detailed breakdown of all changes to Israeli reserve duty (miluim, מילואים) law for 2026, comparing wartime temporary measures with the current permanent law framework.

## Overview

During the wartime period (starting October 2023), the Israeli government enacted several temporary provisions (hora'ot sha'a, הוראות שעה) to support reservists, their families, and employers. Many of these temporary measures have expired or been modified as of 2026. This reference details what has changed, what remains in effect, and what reservists and employers need to know.

## Expired Wartime Provisions

The following measures were enacted as temporary wartime provisions and have expired.

### 1. Extended Post-Service Dismissal Protection

| Aspect | Wartime (Expired) | Current Law (2026) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Duration | 60 days after return from service | 30 days after return from service |
| Applies to | All employers, all reservists | All employers, all reservists |
| Effective change date | Reverted in early 2026 | Permanent law |
| Legal basis | Hora'at Sha'a (temporary provision) | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |

**Impact**: Reservists returning from service now have 30 days of dismissal protection, not 60. Employers can legally terminate a reservist 31 days after their return from service, subject to regular labor law protections.

### 2. Employer Compensation for Social Contributions (20%)

| Aspect | Wartime (Expired) | Current Law (2026) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| What it covered | 20% supplement to employers for pension, keren hishtalmut, and other social contributions during miluim | No supplement |
| Paid by | Government (via Bituach Leumi) | Not applicable |
| Expiration date | January 2026 | Permanent state: employers bear the cost |
| Impact on employers | Reduced financial burden of maintaining employee benefits | Employers pay full social contribution costs during service |

**Impact**: Employers no longer receive the 20% reimbursement for social contributions. They must continue making pension and keren hishtalmut deposits during miluim at their own expense (with reimbursement of salary only via Bituach Leumi).

### 3. Supplementary Employer Compensation

| Aspect | Wartime (Expired) | Current Law (2026) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| What it covered | Additional government payments to employers for costs of maintaining reservist positions | Not available |
| Purpose | Offset hiring temporary replacements, training, and productivity loss | Employers absorb these costs |
| Expiration | 2025 | Not applicable |

### 4. Spouse Unpaid Leave Entitlement

| Aspect | Wartime (Expired) | Current Law (2026) |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| What it provided | Spouses of reservists could take unpaid leave from work during active service | Not in effect |
| Job protection | Employer could not fire spouse for taking this leave | Standard labor law applies |
| Duration | During the reservist's active service period | Not applicable |

### 5. Extended Family Support Services

| Aspect | Wartime (Expired/Modified) | Current Law (2026) |
|--------|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Municipal support level | Enhanced, dedicated services | Standard municipal welfare services |
| School accommodations | Mandatory accommodations for children of reservists | At school's discretion |
| Financial emergency grants | Expanded eligibility and amounts | Standard Bituach Leumi criteria |

## Active Permanent Protections (Unchanged)

These protections are part of permanent law and remain fully in effect.

### Employment Protections

| Protection | Details | Legal Source |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Job preservation during service | Employer must hold the reservist's position | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Same role upon return | Must return to same position, same terms, same pay | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| 30-day post-service dismissal ban | Cannot be fired within 30 days of return | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Pre-service dismissal ban | Cannot be fired because of an upcoming call-up | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Seniority accrual | Service period counts toward work seniority | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Pension contributions | Employer must continue pension deposits during service | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Keren hishtalmut | Employer must continue keren hishtalmut deposits | Reserve Duty Compensation Law |
| Health insurance | Continues during service | National Health Insurance Law |

## New Tax Credit Tiers for 2026

The 2026 tax year introduced updated credit tiers for reservists, replacing the simpler wartime credit structure.

| Days Served Per Year | Credit Tier | Approximate Annual Value | Notes |
|---------------------|-------------|-------------------------|-------|
| 10-30 days | Standard | 1 credit point (nekudat zikui) | Equivalent to approximately 2,904 NIS/year (2026 value) |
| 31-60 days | Enhanced | 1.5 credit points | Approximately 4,356 NIS/year |
| 61-110 days | Higher | 2 credit points | Approximately 5,808 NIS/year |
| 110+ days | Maximum | 2.5 credit points | Approximately 7,260 NIS/year (new tier for 2026) |

**Key changes from wartime credits:**
- The 110+ day tier is new for 2026, reflecting the higher service loads from extended operations
- Credit values are based on the standard credit point value, adjusted annually
- Credits are claimed through the employer (Form 101) or annual tax filing with Rashut HaMisim

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Eligibility | Reservists with monthly income below NIS 9,800 |
| Amount | Approximately NIS 3,000 per month during active service |
| Application | Automatic through Bituach Leumi for eligible reservists |
| Purpose | Compensate for income disruption in lower-income households |
| Duration | During months of active service |

## What Reservists Should Do in 2026

1. **Check your tax credit tier**: Based on days served, ensure your employer is applying the correct credit
2. **File Form 101** with your employer or directly with Rashut HaMisim
3. **Use accumulated wartime vacation days** before end of 2027
4. **Verify pension contributions**: Ensure employer continued deposits during any 2025-2026 service
5. **Self-employed**: File compensation claims promptly with Bituach Leumi
6. **If terminated within 30 days**: Document everything and file a complaint with the Labor Court

## What Employers Should Do in 2026

1. **Update HR policies**: Reflect the 30-day (not 60-day) post-service protection period
2. **Budget for full social contributions**: The 20% government supplement is no longer available
3. **Process Bituach Leumi reimbursements**: File salary reimbursement claims promptly after employee returns
4. **Apply correct tax credits**: Use the new 4-tier credit system based on days served
5. **Track service dates**: Maintain accurate records of employees' service periods using Form 3010
