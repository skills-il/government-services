# Tax Benefits for Olim (New Immigrants) in Israel

This reference provides a detailed guide to the tax exemptions and benefits available to new immigrants (olim chadashim) and veteran returning residents (toshavim chozrim vatikim) in Israel. Consult this guide when advising on tax-related questions for olim.

## Legal Basis

The primary tax benefits for olim are established under:
- **Section 14 of the Income Tax Ordinance** (amendment from 2008): 10-year exemption on foreign income
- **Income Tax Ordinance [New Version]**: Tax credit points for new olim
- **Customs Ordinance**: Duty reductions on personal effects and vehicles

## Who Qualifies

### New Immigrant (Oleh Chadash)

An individual who:
- Made aliyah under the Law of Return
- Was granted oleh status by Misrad HaPnim (Interior Ministry)
- Holds a Teudat Oleh
- Benefits start from the date of aliyah as recorded on the Teudat Oleh

### Veteran Returning Resident (Toshav Chozer Vatik)

An individual who:
- Was an Israeli resident previously
- Lived abroad for at least 10 consecutive years
- Returned to Israel and re-established residency
- Entitled to the same 10-year exemption as olim chadashim

### Regular Returning Resident (Toshav Chozer)

An individual who:
- Lived abroad for at least 6 consecutive years (but fewer than 10)
- Receives reduced benefits (not the full 10-year exemption)
- Different rules apply; consult a tax advisor for specifics

## 10-Year Foreign Income Exemption (Section 14)

This is the most significant tax benefit for olim.

### What Is Exempt

| Income Type | Exempt? | Duration | Notes |
|-------------|---------|----------|-------|
| Foreign employment income | Yes | 10 years | Income earned outside Israel |
| Foreign pension | Yes | 10 years | Pensions from foreign employers or governments |
| Foreign rental income | Yes | 10 years | Rent from properties owned abroad |
| Foreign dividends | Yes | 10 years | Dividends from foreign companies |
| Foreign interest | Yes | 10 years | Interest from foreign bank accounts and investments |
| Foreign capital gains | Yes | 10 years | Gains from selling foreign assets |
| Foreign business income | Yes | 10 years | Profits from businesses operated abroad |
| Israeli employment income | No | N/A | Taxed normally from day one |
| Israeli business income | No | N/A | Taxed normally from day one |
| Israeli rental income | No | N/A | Taxed normally |

### What Is NOT Exempt

- Income from Israeli sources (salary from Israeli employer, Israeli rental income, Israeli business)
- Income from assets acquired in Israel after aliyah
- Income from Israeli financial instruments

### Reporting Requirements

- **Olim arriving before January 1, 2026**: No reporting obligation for exempt foreign income (both tax and reporting exemptions apply)
- **Olim arriving from January 1, 2026 onwards**: Must report all worldwide income and assets, even if the income is tax-exempt under the 10-year exemption. The reporting exemption was abolished while the tax exemption remains
- **Israeli-source income**: Must be reported and taxed normally from day one
- **Annual tax return (doch shnati)**: Required if total taxable income (Israeli-source) exceeds the filing threshold, or if reporting obligations apply per the 2026 rules

### Key Rules

1. **10-year clock**: Starts from the date of aliyah, not the calendar year
2. **No proration**: The exemption applies for full 10 years regardless of when in the year aliyah occurred
3. **No cap**: There is no maximum amount of exempt foreign income
4. **Asset classification**: The location of the income source (not where it is received) determines if it is foreign
5. **Foreign company ownership**: Olim who own or control foreign companies may have special reporting considerations. Consult a tax professional.

## Tax Credit Points (Nekudot Zikui)

Olim receive additional tax credit points that reduce their Israeli tax liability.

### Credit Point Schedule (reformed in 2022, branch on the aliyah date)

**Arrived 2022 or later: 8.5 points over 54 months** (Income Tax Ordinance s.35).

| Months since aliyah | Points per month | Cumulative points | Monthly value (2026) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-12 | 1/12 of an annual point | 1.0 | 242 NIS |
| 13-30 | 1/4 | 4.5 | 726 NIS |
| 31-42 | 1/6 | 2.0 | 484 NIS |
| 43-54 | 1/12 | 1.0 | 242 NIS |

**Arrived before 2022: the legacy ladder, 7.5 points over 42 months** (18 months at 1/4, 12 at 1/6, 12 at 1/12). It has no opening 1/12 period.

Regular IDF service and post-secondary study do NOT count toward the 54 months: the clock pauses and the unused remainder resumes on discharge or graduation. This is automatic, not an election. In every other case the clock runs continuously from the teudat oleh, including months with no income, and those months are lost.

### How It Works

- One credit point is worth NIS 242 per month / NIS 2,904 per year in 2026 (Israel Tax Authority, monthly deductions booklet, "נקודת זיכוי 33א 242 לחודש"). The value is updated annually.
- Credit points reduce the tax owed, not the taxable income
- If the total credit points exceed the tax liability, the excess is not refunded (for employment income)
- Credit points apply automatically when the employer is notified of oleh status via Form 101
- Self-employed olim claim credit points through annual tax returns

### Total Benefit

Full use of the post-2022 ladder is worth about NIS 24,700 of tax (12 x 242 + 18 x 726 + 12 x 484 + 12 x 242, at the 2026 credit-point value). The legacy 42-month ladder is worth about NIS 21,800. These oleh points are on top of the 2.25 resident points.

## Customs and Import Benefits

### Personal Belongings

- Olim can import personal belongings **duty-free** within defined time windows
- "Personal belongings" includes furniture, clothing, household items, electronics, books
- Items must have been owned for at least 6 months before aliyah
- Import window: typically within 3 years of aliyah (some items have shorter windows)

### Vehicle Import

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Reduced customs duty | Significant reduction on one vehicle |
| Eligibility | Within 3 years of aliyah date |
| Vehicle age | Restrictions on age of imported vehicle |
| Resale restriction | Cannot sell the vehicle for a defined period without paying back the duty discount |

Important notes on vehicles:
- The exact discount percentage changes periodically
- Insurance in Israel is typically more expensive than many other countries
- Consider whether importing a vehicle is cost-effective vs. buying locally
- Israeli roads use kilometers, and some signage conventions differ

### Appliances and Electronics

- One set of major household appliances may be imported duty-free
- Includes: refrigerator, washing machine, dryer, dishwasher, oven, TV
- Must be for personal use, not for resale
- Time limits apply (typically within first year of aliyah)

## Property Purchase Tax (Mas Rechisha)

### Standard Benefit

Olim buying a single residential home in Israel get a reduced mas rechisha (purchase tax) rate under the reformed Purchase Tax Regulation 12a (the track in force from 15 August 2024). 2026 brackets (frozen at 2025 levels):

| Property value (NIS) | Standard (single home) | Oleh (Reg 12a) |
|----------------------|------------------------|----------------|
| 0 - 1,978,745 | 0% | 0% |
| 1,978,745 - 2,347,040 | 3.5% | 0.5% |
| 2,347,040 - 6,055,070 | 5% | 0.5% |
| 6,055,070 - 20,183,565 | 8% | 8% |
| 20,183,565+ | 10% | 10% |

The reform lowered the whole 1,978,745 to 6,055,070 band to 0.5% for olim (the older track charged 5% on the part above 2,347,040). The tax is tiered: each rate applies only to the portion of the price inside its band, not to the entire value.

### Eligibility

- Granted once only, and only for a single residential home (not an investment apartment)
- Eligibility window: from one year before aliyah to seven years after the aliyah date
- The reformed track no longer requires the home to be the oleh's actual residence
- Exact thresholds are updated periodically by the Israel Tax Authority; verify before relying on them

### Process

1. Sign purchase agreement with the seller
2. Lawyer (orech din) files mas rechisha declaration within 30 days
3. Include proof of oleh status (Teudat Oleh) in the filing
4. Reduced rate applied automatically upon verification

## VAT Considerations

- Standard VAT rate in Israel is 18% (raised from 17% effective January 1, 2025)
- Olim do NOT receive a general VAT exemption
- Some specific programs may offer VAT reductions on first-purchase appliances (check current eligibility)
- Imported personal belongings in the duty-free container are also exempt from VAT

## Social Security Considerations (Bituach Leumi)

While not strictly "tax" benefits, Bituach Leumi contributions interact with the tax system:

| Factor | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Employee contributions | Deducted from salary, partially tax-deductible |
| Self-employed contributions | Must pay directly, calculated on reported income |
| Reduced rates for new olim | May apply for the first period (verify current rules) |
| Health tax (mas briut) | Included in Bituach Leumi contributions |

## Common Mistakes and Pitfalls

1. **Assuming all income is exempt**: Only FOREIGN-source income is exempt. Israeli income is taxed from day one.

2. **Not opening a tax file**: Even with the exemption, olim who are self-employed or have Israeli-source income above the threshold must open a tax file with the Israel Tax Authority (petihat tik).

3. **Missing the property purchase window**: The mas rechisha discount expires 7 years after aliyah. Plan property purchases accordingly.

4. **Not notifying employers**: Employers must be informed of oleh status (via Form 101, tofes 101) to apply the additional credit points. Without notification, standard tax rates apply and you must file for a refund.

5. **Ignoring tax treaty obligations**: Israel has tax treaties with many countries. The 10-year exemption does not override foreign tax obligations. Income taxed in Israel may be creditable in the foreign country, and vice versa.

6. **Selling imported goods prematurely**: Duty-free imports have resale restrictions. Selling imported items (especially vehicles) before the restriction period ends requires repayment of the customs benefit.

7. **Confusing residency status**: Tax residency is determined by the "center of life" test (merkaz chayim). Moving back and forth between countries can create complex residency questions.

## When to Consult a Professional

Consult a tax advisor (yo'etz mas) specializing in olim if you:
- Have significant foreign assets or investments
- Own or control foreign companies
- Receive income from multiple countries
- Are self-employed with both Israeli and foreign income
- Plan to purchase property in Israel
- Are a returning resident and unsure of your exact status category
- Have complex family situations affecting tax filing (e.g., spouse remains abroad)

### Finding a Tax Advisor

- Misrad HaKlita can provide referrals to advisors experienced with olim
- Nefesh B'Nefesh (for English-speaking olim) maintains a list of recommended advisors
- The Israel Bar Association and Council of CPAs have online directories
- Verify the advisor is licensed (yo'etz mas musmach) before engaging

## New-oleh Israeli-source earned-income exemption (2026 incentive window)

A new tax incentive grants olim and toshavim chozrim vatikim who establish Israeli residency between **November 5, 2025 and December 31, 2026** a tiered exemption on **Israeli-source earned income** (employment salary and self-employment only, NOT passive income), on top of the standard 10-year foreign-income exemption.

| Tax year | Exemption ceiling on earned Israeli-source income |
|---|---|
| 2026 | up to NIS 600,000 |
| 2027 | up to NIS 1,000,000 |
| 2028 | up to NIS 1,000,000 |
| 2029 | up to NIS 350,000 |
| 2030 | up to NIS 150,000 |

- **Related-party cap:** when working for a related party (own company, family business), the exemption is capped at NIS 140,000 per year for all years 2026-2030.
- **75-day residency requirement:** eligibility is lost if you spend fewer than 75 days in Israel during 2028 or 2029, or cease being an Israeli tax resident in those years.
- **Earned income only:** rental, interest, dividends do NOT qualify. The standard Section 14 ten-year foreign-income exemption remains intact.
- Olim arriving outside the November 5, 2025 - December 31, 2026 window do NOT receive this incentive, only the standard Section 14 benefits.

Source: Shivat Zion 501000361627 (Tax Reform 2026).
