# Aliyah Additional Procedures and Edge Cases

This reference covers procedural details and edge cases that the main SKILL.md body points to. Consult per topic.

## A1 vs Oleh visa comparison

When opening a file with the Jewish Agency, the eligible candidate picks between two visa paths. They cover different intents.

| Dimension | A1 (Temporary Resident) | Oleh (Immigrant) |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | Trial period for someone eligible to make aliyah but uncertain | Immediate aliyah under the Law of Return |
| Validity | 1 year, renewable annually | Permanent (becomes citizen on arrival) |
| Aliyah benefits (Sal Klita, free Ulpan, customs duty-free, tax exemptions) | Not eligible | Full basket |
| Health insurance | Must obtain private insurance; Bituach Leumi contributions required after 6 months | Immediate sal briut via kupat cholim |
| Israeli passport (Darkon) | Not issued | NOT issued immediately. Olim receive a **Teudat Ma'avar** (one-year travel document) until residency intent is demonstrated (typically 1 year in Israel + fees). The full Darkon comes after that. Some countries do not accept Teudat Ma'avar; plan international trips accordingly. |
| Military service obligation | None | Standard rules apply by age and gender; see "IDF and Lone Soldier" below |
| Switch path | Can convert to oleh status later via Shinui Ma'amad (see below) | One-way (ARLI within 3 months to renounce) |

Source: Shivat Zion knowledge base 501000348808.

## Shinui Ma'amad (Aliyah from within Israel)

A person already physically inside Israel on a tourist visa, A1 temporary residence, student visa, work visa, or family-reunification visa can change status to oleh chadash without leaving and re-entering. This is called Shinui Ma'amad ("status change").

**Who uses this path:**
- People who entered as tourists and decided to stay
- A1 holders who completed their trial year and want full aliyah benefits
- Students who finished university and want to settle
- Family members joining an Israeli spouse who decided on full aliyah

**Procedure:**
1. Open a file with the Jewish Agency Israel office (not the consulate abroad)
2. Submit the standard aliyah document set: proof of Jewish identity, passport, marriage/birth certificates with apostille
3. **From November 2024**: present background checks from every country lived in for 6+ months (broader than the 1-year-after-age-14 standard rule)
4. File the status-change request at Misrad HaPnim (Population and Immigration Authority)
5. Receive Teudat Oleh and Teudat Zehut once approved

**Note:** Step 3 ("At Ben Gurion Airport") in the main skill does NOT apply to Shinui Ma'amad applicants. The Teudat Oleh and initial Sal Klita payment are issued at Misrad HaPnim and Misrad HaKlita branches inside Israel, not at the airport.

Source: Shivat Zion 501000348703 (background-check rule); the main Shinui Ma'amad procedure follows the same document set as standard aliyah.

## Converts (giyur) document set

In addition to the standard aliyah document list, an oleh who converted to Judaism must provide four letters. The Jewish Agency Rabbinate Department reviews these separately and may request more.

**Required letters:**

1. **Letter on Pre-Conversion Preparation** from the rabbi who guided the conversion. Describes why and when the applicant approached the congregation, when they first met the rabbi, and the preparation process. Dated, ink-signed, stamped with the rabbi's or community's seal.
2. **Letter on Communal Participation** describing the applicant's involvement in the Jewish community before, during, and after conversion, with specific activities. Same dating/signing/stamping requirements.
3. **Letter from External Beit Din** (if applicable) when conversion was performed by a beit din outside the congregation in which the applicant studied. Documents the beit din's authority and the conversion certificate.
4. **Personal Letter from Applicant** describing motivation for conversion, when and how the conversion process started, and details of study. Dated and ink-signed by the applicant.

The Shivat Zion source does not enumerate different requirements per stream (Orthodox / Conservative / Reform). The Jewish Agency or Misrad HaPnim may ask for additional letters case-by-case.

Source: Shivat Zion 501000348706.

## Aliyah BeNifrad / Split Aliyah

Scenario: one family member makes aliyah first, with spouse and/or children under 21 joining within less than one year.

**Sal Klita implication:** Sal Klita is calculated per family unit, not per individual. The first arriving group receives the bulk of the family allocation. The later-arriving members receive only the remainder.

Shivat Zion illustrates the shape (not actual figures): a family entitled to a total Sal Klita amount that is split gives the larger share to the first arriving member and the remainder to the second, rather than treating each arrival as a fresh family unit. The exact amounts depend on the Sal Klita rate Misrad HaKlita publishes for that year.

**Practical guidance for advisors:**
- If timing is flexible, recommend the family arrive within a few weeks of each other so the full basket is paid to whoever opens the bank account first
- If the split is unavoidable (e.g., one spouse must finish a work contract abroad), the family should know in advance that the second arrival will not refresh the basket
- Each adult should still register independently with Misrad HaPnim for Teudat Zehut

Source: Shivat Zion 501000348807.

## Other edge-case applicants

| Category | What changes vs. standard aliyah |
|---|---|
| **Special-needs adults aged 21+** | Adults with disabilities aged 21+ are not automatically included in a parent's family file. They need their own aliyah file and may need a guardian (apotropus). Misrad HaKlita has a dedicated absorption track for adults with disabilities. The Jewish Agency processes the eligibility check; sal klita and housing assistance differ. |
| **Adopted children** | Must include adoption decree with apostille from the country where adoption was finalized. If the adoption is not recognized under Israeli law, additional Beit Din involvement may be required for the child to gain citizenship under the Law of Return. |
| **Pets (dogs and cats)** | Permit required from the Veterinary Services. Dogs: minimum age 4 months, rabies vaccination at least 12 months and at most 1 month before travel, rabies antibody titer ≥0.5 IU/ml from an approved lab. Dangerous breeds (American Staffordshire Terrier, Bull Terrier, Pit Bull, Rottweiler, Tosa, Argentinian Dogo, Brazilian Mastiff) are restricted. Approved ports: Ben Gurion, Haifa, Ashdod, Eilat. |

## MyGov personal account

In the first week, every new oleh should create a personal account on the gov.il portal (commonly called "MyGov" or "Ezor Ishi Ezrach"). This single account unlocks:
- Bituach Leumi self-service (claim status, contribution history)
- Misrad HaKlita personal area (sal klita payment status)
- Rashut HaMisim self-service (tax filings, tax credit point check)
- Misrad HaPnim services (passport application, Teudat Zehut renewal)
- Misrad HaTachbura (driving license, motor vehicle records)

Sign up at `gov.il` using the Teudat Zehut + a SIM-card-verifiable Israeli phone number. Identity verification is via SMS one-time-code.

## Refund of government payments paid before Teudat Oleh

Olim sometimes pay government fees before they hold a Teudat Oleh: passport processing in the home country, document apostille fees, consular fees. Some of these are reimbursable by Misrad HaKlita after aliyah. The reimbursement is partial and requires original receipts. File the request at the absorbing Misrad HaKlita branch within the first year. Bituach Leumi contributions wrongly charged before sal briut activation are also refundable. The Israel Tax Authority refunds overpaid income tax automatically when the employer applies Form 101 with oleh status.

## Bringing Money declaration thresholds (Customs)

Cash, financial instruments, and bearer securities brought into Israel must be declared at customs if the total value exceeds the declaration threshold.

- **Standard threshold (non-bank account holder):** declaration required for cash and bearer instruments equivalent to NIS 50,000 or more
- **Israeli citizen / oleh entering for the first time:** higher threshold (currently NIS 100,000 equivalent for olim per ITA guidance)
- Declaration form is filed at customs at the port of entry
- Verify current threshold at the Israel Tax Authority customs page before advising; thresholds are subject to periodic adjustment under Money Laundering law

Failing to declare incurs civil penalties (typically a percentage of the undeclared amount) and may trigger investigation under Money Laundering Prohibition Law 2000.

## Buying a car with olim benefits (3-year window)

Olim can purchase a car in Israel with reduced purchase-tax benefits within 3 years of aliyah. The benefit applies to:
- A new car bought in Israel from an authorized dealer
- A used car bought from a dealer (subject to the same window)
- A car imported by the oleh in their duty-free container

**Conditions:**
- The car must be registered in the oleh's name
- Cannot be sold within 4 years of registration without paying back the customs/purchase-tax discount
- One car per oleh family
- Vehicle class is typically restricted to M1 (passenger cars)

Eligibility is verified at the dealer using the Teudat Oleh. Compare the all-in cost (Israel-purchase with oleh benefits vs. importing) before deciding.

## Child Savings Plan (Chisachon LeKol Yeled)

Bituach Leumi opens a savings plan automatically for every Israeli child from birth up to age 18. For olim, the plan is opened upon Teudat Zehut issuance for each child under 18.

- Monthly deposit (currently around NIS 55 per child, indexed) is added by Bituach Leumi
- Parents can elect to double the deposit by adding their own NIS 55, withdrawn automatically from child allowance
- Parents choose between a bank savings account (lower risk) or a kupat gemel (long-term investment fund); default is bank account for under-21-month children
- Funds are released to the child at age 18 (or earlier with parental consent + age 15)
- Olim parents should activate the plan via the Bituach Leumi personal area within the first month

## IDF and Lone Soldier (Chayal Boded) status

Young olim are subject to Israeli military service. The skill does NOT replace dedicated draft / Chayal Boded guidance, but a complete aliyah workflow must name the touchpoints.

**Who is drafted:**
- Men: ages 18 to 22 at arrival are subject to immediate Tzav Rishon (first draft notice) and induction
- Women: ages 18 to 20 at arrival are subject to draft, with broader exemption / national-service paths
- Older olim get reduced service obligations or full exemption based on age and family status

**Tzav Rishon:** the IDF Meitav unit issues a first-call letter for medical exam and profile assessment. Olim receive theirs once Misrad HaPnim registration completes. Tzav Rishon does NOT mean immediate induction; it is the start of a multi-step process.

**Deferrals (dechiyat sherut):** available for academic study (typically engineering, medicine), yeshiva, or new oleh adjustment (up to 1 year). Apply to the IDF Meitav unit with supporting documentation.

**Chayal Boded (Lone Soldier) status:** olim who serve without parents resident in Israel receive a named legal status with concrete benefits, including extra salary supplement, one paid month off per year to visit family abroad, IDF-covered round-trip flight, dedicated counseling at Mador HaBoded, special housing subsidy, and a Misrad HaBitachon end-of-service grant. The Lone Soldier basket is comparable in scope to Sal Klita and should be requested explicitly at induction.

**Exemptions (ptor):** medical (profile 21), mental-health, religious (Torato Umanuto), and conscientious objector tracks each have separate procedures. Verify with the IDF Meitav unit or a specialized advisor.

For comprehensive IDF guidance for olim, refer young users to Misrad HaBitachon (Ministry of Defense) and the Lone Soldier Center (lonesoldiercenter.com / nefeshbenefesh.org/lone-soldier).

## Conversion-stream recognition gap (Reform / Conservative converts)

This is one of the largest post-aliyah surprises and the converts document set above does NOT warn about it explicitly. Misrad HaPnim recognizes Reform and Conservative conversions performed abroad for purposes of the Law of Return (the oleh receives Teudat Oleh and Teudat Zehut normally). However, the Israeli Rabbanut HaRashit (Chief Rabbinate) does NOT recognize Reform or Conservative conversions for marriage, divorce, burial in Orthodox cemeteries, or other religious-court matters.

**Practical consequences for Reform/Conservative converts:**
- Cannot marry in Israel under Rabbanut auspices; must marry abroad (typically Cyprus, Prague) and register the foreign marriage at Misrad HaPnim
- Custody and inheritance complications if the spouse is Jewish-by-Rabbanut and the convert is not
- If citizenship is later renounced and Aliyah BaShenit pursued, the Rabbanut recognition gap persists into the second cycle
- Marriage to a Jew-by-Rabbanut may require the convert to undergo Orthodox conversion in Israel (giyur k'halacha) for the marriage to be recognized

This is a status split, not a bureaucratic delay. Advise Reform / Conservative converts to consult ITIM (itim.org.il), the leading Israeli organization handling Rabbanut recognition issues, BEFORE making major life decisions tied to Jewish identity in Israel.

## School and gan enrollment for olim children

Israeli law requires children ages 3-18 to be enrolled in education within ~30 days of arrival. This is a week-1 task for families with school-age children, on par with kupat cholim registration.

**Where to enroll:**
- Ages 3-6 (gan/kindergarten): apply at the local municipality (iriya) education department
- Ages 6-18 (beit sefer): apply at the local municipality education department; placement is by neighborhood for elementary, by choice for high school

**Misrad HaChinuch oleh program (Sela):**
- **Kitot Kelita** (absorption classes): intensive Hebrew + bridging curriculum for olim children, typically 1-2 years
- **Extra Hebrew hours:** entitled separately from Kitot Kelita
- **Free supplementary teacher** for math and English: 2 years of free private tutoring funded by Misrad HaChinuch
- **Sela inscription window:** typically open year-round for new arrivals; mid-year enrollment is allowed

**For a family arriving mid-academic-year:** the municipality must enroll the child within 30 days. If a school refuses citing capacity, escalate to Misrad HaChinuch's regional aliyah absorption coordinator. The right to enroll is statutory.

**For special-education needs:** the local committee (Vaadat Hashma'a) evaluates within 60 days; bring foreign IEP / evaluations with apostille + Hebrew translation.

## Citizenship-status verification before status changes

Before any change in family status (marriage, divorce, birth, change of citizenship, ARLI declaration), an oleh should verify their citizenship status at Misrad HaPnim. A common mistake: an oleh who naturalized as a US citizen post-aliyah and assumed it was automatically reported to Misrad HaPnim. Some agencies (Bituach Leumi for pension calculations, IDF for service obligation) cross-check the citizenship-status field. An out-of-date record can cause benefit suspensions.

Verify by logging into the gov.il personal account → Misrad HaPnim → "Citizenship Status" page. Discrepancies should be reported with supporting documentation (foreign naturalization certificate with apostille).
