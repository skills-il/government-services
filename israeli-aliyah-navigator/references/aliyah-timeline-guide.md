# Aliyah Timeline Guide: Month-by-Month First Year

This reference provides a detailed month-by-month breakdown of the first year after making aliyah to Israel. Use this guide to build a long-term plan and ensure no critical deadlines are missed.

## Month 1: Arrival and Setup

### Week 1: Immediate Priorities

| Day | Task | Location | Notes |
|-----|------|----------|-------|
| Day 1 | Receive Teudat Oleh at airport | Ben Gurion, Misrad HaKlita desk | Carry all original documents |
| Day 1 | Collect initial sal klita cash | Ben Gurion | First payment in cash |
| Day 1 | Get free SIM card | Airport arrivals | Basic Israeli number |
| Day 1-2 | Register at Misrad HaPnim | Local branch | Bring passport, photos, Teudat Oleh |
| Day 2-3 | Open bank account | Major bank branch | Teudat Oleh + passport required |
| Day 2-3 | Join kupat cholim | Clalit/Maccabi/Meuhedet/Leumit | Choose based on services and location |
| Day 3-5 | Register at Bituach Leumi | Local branch or btl.gov.il | Triggers health insurance |

### Week 2-4: Settling In

- Receive Teudat Zehut (Israeli ID card) by mail or at Misrad HaPnim
- Set up online banking and mobile app
- Arrange temporary or permanent housing
- Sign rental contract (if renting)
- Apply for arnona (municipal tax) discount at local municipality
- Register for Ulpan through Misrad HaKlita or municipality
- Set up utilities: electricity (IEC), water, internet, gas
- Get Rav-Kav card for public transportation
- Register with local post office for mail delivery
- For families: register children at local school or gan (kindergarten)

### Key Deadlines

- Bank account: open within first week to receive sal klita deposits
- Kupat cholim: register within first week for health coverage
- Bituach Leumi: register within first two weeks

## Month 2: Building Routine

### Priorities

- Begin Ulpan classes (if not started in Month 1)
- Verify first monthly sal klita payment received in bank account
- Set up standing orders (horaot keva) for rent and recurring bills
- Start job search or register with Misrad HaKlita employment services
- Begin driver's license conversion process:
  - Get medical fitness certificate
  - Submit application at licensing office (misrad harishui)
  - If theory test needed: start studying for it
- Explore local services: supermarkets, clinics, pharmacies
- For professionals: begin gathering documents for license recognition

### Financial Tasks

- Monitor sal klita payments monthly
- Set up systematic savings if possible
- Research Israeli pension options (keren pensia)
- Understand Israeli bank fees and consider negotiating with bank

## Month 3: Integration

### Priorities

- Continue Ulpan (should be well into the course by now)
- Active job searching or employment counseling
- For medical/legal/accounting professionals: submit recognition applications
- Complete driver's license theory test (if required)
- Start building Israeli references and professional network
- Explore professional retraining programs (hachshara miktzoit) via Misrad HaKlita

### Housing

- If in temporary housing: begin searching for permanent apartment
- Understand rental market norms:
  - Lease terms typically 12 months with option to renew
  - Rent increases capped by linked index (madad)
  - Security deposits or bank guarantees standard
  - Post-dated checks (chekim dechuyim) for rent

## Month 4-5: Building Momentum

### Career Development

- Network with Israeli professionals in your field
- Attend industry meetups and events
- Update LinkedIn for Israeli job market
- Consider volunteering in your professional field for experience
- For tech professionals: explore Israeli tech ecosystem (meetups, accelerators)

### Administrative Follow-ups

- Check status of professional license applications
- Verify Bituach Leumi contributions are current
- If employed: ensure employer is making proper deductions (pension, bituach leumi)
- Review kupat cholim supplementary insurance options

### Driver's License

- For countries with full recognition: should have Israeli license by now
- For theory test countries: take theory test if not done
- For full testing: complete driving lessons and schedule practical test

## Month 6: Mid-Year Review

### Sal Klita

- Final monthly payment for singles (Month 6)
- Couples and families receive one more payment (Month 7)
- Verify total received matches expected amount
- Report any discrepancies to Misrad HaKlita

### Assessment Checklist

| Area | Status Check |
|------|-------------|
| Hebrew | Should be at conversational level from Ulpan |
| Employment | Employed or actively in job search process |
| Housing | In permanent housing with lease |
| Banking | All financial services set up and running |
| Health | Kupat cholim active, supplementary considered |
| License | Conversion complete or in final stages |
| Professional | Recognition application submitted and in progress |
| Social | Building local social network |

## Month 7-9: Deepening Roots

### Career

- If employed: focus on establishing yourself at work
- If job searching: consider broadening search criteria
- Explore Israeli workplace culture norms
- Build professional Hebrew vocabulary
- For professionals awaiting recognition: follow up on applications

### Financial Planning

- Review and understand Israeli tax obligations
- Consider consulting a tax advisor specializing in olim
- If receiving foreign income: understand 10-year exemption rules
- Start retirement planning (keren pensia is mandatory for employees)

### Ulpan Completion

- Complete Ulpan Aleph (500 hours)
- Consider continuing to Ulpan Bet for further improvement
- Practice Hebrew in daily life and at work
- Read Israeli news in Hebrew (even partially)

## Month 10-11: Looking Ahead

### Administrative

- Renew arnona discount for second year (some municipalities auto-renew)
- Review health insurance: consider changing kupat cholim during transfer period
- Verify all Bituach Leumi rights are active
- Check if eligible for any additional Misrad HaKlita programs

### Long-term Planning

- Evaluate whether to renew current lease or relocate
- Begin thinking about property purchase (mas rechisha discount for olim)
- Review pension contributions and options
- For professionals: check progress on license recognition

## Month 12: First Anniversary

### Key Milestones

| Milestone | Deadline | Action if Not Complete |
|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| Driver's license conversion | 12 months from aliyah | Foreign license expires, may need full testing |
| Ulpan Aleph completion | 18 months from aliyah | Still eligible, but start soon |
| Sal klita | Should be complete | Contact Misrad HaKlita if payments missing |
| Arnona discount | Reapply annually | Visit municipality for renewal |

### Year-End Review

- Assess Hebrew level and plan continued learning
- File annual tax return (doch shnati) if required
- Review all active benefits and entitlements
- Set goals for Year 2: career advancement, Hebrew fluency, community integration
- Consider permanent residency implications if not yet addressed

## Critical Deadlines Summary

| Deadline | Timeframe | Consequence of Missing |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Bank account opening | First week | Delayed sal klita payments |
| Bituach Leumi registration | First month | Gap in health coverage |
| Ulpan start | Within 18 months | Lose free 500-hour entitlement |
| Driver's license conversion | Within 12 months | Foreign license becomes invalid |
| Sal klita registration | Within 12 months | May forfeit remaining payments |
| Professional license application | As soon as possible | Delayed career progression |
| Arnona discount renewal | Annually | Pay full municipal tax |

## Tips for a Successful First Year

1. **Do not delay bank account opening.** Many processes depend on having an Israeli bank account.
2. **Start Ulpan as early as possible.** Hebrew proficiency dramatically affects quality of life and employment.
3. **Keep all documents organized.** Create a folder system for Teudat Oleh, bank documents, lease, Bituach Leumi correspondence.
4. **Use the Misrad HaKlita personal area** (ezor ishi at klita.gov.il) to track benefits and deadlines.
5. **Build a support network.** Connect with other olim, join community groups, attend local events.
6. **Be patient with bureaucracy.** Israeli government offices can be slow. Follow up regularly but allow processing time.
7. **Leverage oleh discounts.** Arnona, mas rechisha, and other benefits can save significant money.
8. **Consult professionals** for tax and legal matters, especially if you have foreign assets or complex income situations.
