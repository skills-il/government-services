# Driver's License Conversion for Olim

This reference covers the procedural details of converting a foreign driver's license. Main SKILL.md Step 10 summarizes the high-level path; consult this file for the full sub-procedures.

## Experience-based tiers (the rule replaced country-of-origin in August 2017)

| Driving Experience | Process | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 5+ years consecutive | Administrative conversion only (no tests) | Applies to licenses from USA, Canada, UK, EU, Australia, South Africa, and other recognized countries |
| 2 to 5 years | Short practical test (mivchan shlita) | No theory test required |
| Less than 2 years | Full testing (theory + practical) | May need driving lessons |

## Conversion process (5+ years experience)

1. Obtain a medical fitness certificate (ishur refui) from a licensed physician
2. Submit application at a licensing office (misrad harishui) with:
   - Valid foreign license (with certified Hebrew translation)
   - Teudat Zehut
   - Medical certificate
   - Passport photos
3. Receive Israeli license (usually within 2-4 weeks)

## Conversion process (2-5 years experience)

1. Same documents as 5-year process
2. Pass a short practical driving test (mivchan shlita)
3. No theory test required
4. Schedule at any testing center via the misrad harishui website

## Conversion process (less than 2 years experience)

1. Take driving lessons at a licensed school (beit sefer lenehiga); typically 28 lessons minimum
2. Pass theory test (available in Hebrew, English, Russian, Arabic, Amharic, French)
3. Pass practical driving test
4. Theory test covers Israeli-specific road rules and signage

## Temporary driving window

- Foreign license valid for up to 1 year from your **last entry to Israel**. Clock resets if you spend 6+ months continuously abroad.
- Conversion window is **5 years from aliyah date** (not 1 year). After 5 years without conversion you must complete a full Israeli licensing process (theory + practical), regardless of prior experience.
- Must carry both the foreign license and Teudat Oleh/Zehut while driving.
- Start the conversion early to avoid driving-eligibility gaps when international trips reset the foreign-license clock.

## Theory and practical test languages

Theory test is available in 6 languages: Hebrew, English, Russian, Arabic, Amharic, French. The practical test is conducted in Hebrew, but examiners can give basic instructions in English on request. Bring an interpreter if more support is needed.

## Common rejection reasons

- Foreign license expired before the application was submitted
- Translation not certified by a Notary (notarion) recognized in Israel
- Photos do not meet the misrad harishui photo specs (white background, recent, specific dimensions)
- Medical certificate older than 6 months at submission
