## Documentation typically required (varies per embassy)

- Israeli passport with ≥6 months remaining and 2 blank pages
- Criminal record extract from the Israeli Police, apostilled at the Israeli MFA Authentication Branch (or via my.gov.il e-apostille)
- Salary slips or income proof in the original currency (last 3-6 months minimum)
- Bank statements showing required savings/income
- Translated lease or accommodation booking
- Employment contract or proof of self-employment (osek murshe certificate apostilled)
- For DNV programs: university degree (or proof of 3+ years of relevant experience as a substitute) and private health insurance with required coverage tiers
- Schengen ETIAS pre-authorization (~€20, valid 3 years) for application travel from late 2026 onwards
- Some embassies require Hebrew↔English↔target-language tri-language translation chain with separate apostilles

## Schengen 90/180 management

For Israelis cycling through Schengen countries during the off-season of a non-Schengen primary visa, the standard 90-day-in-180 visa-free rule still applies and is enforced electronically via EES from late 2026. Common rotations:

- Thailand DTV (180 days) → Spain Schengen visa-free (90 days) → buffer in Georgia or UAE → return cycle
- Portugal D8 residency (no Schengen days consumed) → flexible Schengen travel under residency
- Georgia year-round (visa-free) ↔ Schengen quarters (use the 90/180 budget)
