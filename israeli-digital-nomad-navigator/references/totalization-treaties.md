## Countries NOT on the list (high-traffic Israeli-nomad destinations)

This list catches the most common mistake: assuming Form A1 will help. It will NOT for any of these - there is no totalization treaty in force.

- **United States** - no totalization. An Israeli employee on Israeli payroll working from the US cannot avoid US Social Security via A1.
- **Spain** - no totalization. (Israel and Spain are in negotiations as of 2026 but no agreement is in force.)
- **Portugal** - no totalization.
- **Mexico, Costa Rica, Georgia, UAE, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Thailand, Indonesia, Singapore, Vietnam, Croatia, Estonia (separate from EU posted-worker rules), Hungary, Cyprus, Malta** - none have full totalization with Israel.

If any of these destinations is chosen, the Israeli employee on Israeli payroll faces double social-security exposure (Israeli bituach leumi via the Israeli payroll AND the host country's social-security charge on the same wages, where applicable). Plan the cash impact and explore voluntary local-only payment routes if available.

## Form A1 / Certificate of Coverage procedure

For destinations that ARE on the totalization list:

1. **Apply BEFORE departure** at the Bituach Leumi self-service portal or via the international affairs office. The certificate is normally issued within 2-4 weeks.
2. **Required documents:** employment contract proving the Israeli employer-employee relationship, dates of work abroad (start + intended end), foreign-employer host details if applicable, Israeli employee ID + Bituach Leumi number.
3. **Issued document:** Certificate of Coverage (Israeli equivalent of EU Form A1 / US-treaty Form CON 1/2). Standard validity is up to 24 months for posted workers; extendable in some treaty contexts.
4. **Use:** present to the foreign social-security authority on arrival (or proactively) to be exempted from local contributions on the same wages.
5. **Renewal:** if the stay extends beyond the certificate's validity, file for renewal before expiry. Late filing leaves a gap that the foreign authority may charge for retroactively.

## Old-age pension (קיצבת זקנה) collection from a treaty country

For long-term nomads or early retirees who cross the kitzbat zikna eligibility age while abroad: bilateral conventions allow direct payment of the Israeli pension into a bank account in the host country. Non-treaty countries require ongoing eligibility checks and may require return-to-Israel for collection. Reference: btl.gov.il "Old Age Insurance - Staying abroad - Treaty country" page.
