# Domain Checklist: Israeli Returning Resident Customs and Vehicle Planner

Scope: customs benefits for Israelis returning home (toshav chozer / סטודנט חוזר), specifically (a) personal-effects household-shipment customs (purchase tax + VAT + duty exemption) and (b) the personal vehicle import benefit. Planning aid that produces a customs declaration draft, a vehicle decision worksheet, and a windows timeline. NOT the process navigator and NOT the tax math.

Last research date: 2026-05-23. The gov.il customs guide for returning residents was last updated 18.05.2026, so the numbers below are fresh.

## Must cover (core facts the skill must surface in any meaningful answer)

1. **Returnee customs status, who qualifies (Meches definition, NOT the Misrad HaAliyah certificate definition)**
   - "Returning resident" for customs purposes: Israeli resident who was abroad **2 years or more** and is returning to Israel permanently. Also covers second-time olim and "other entrants" the Israel Tax Authority specifically classifies as returnees ([gov.il customs guide for returning residents](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents), section "תושבים חוזרים", updated 18.05.2026).
   - "Returning student" (סטודנט חוזר): Israeli resident abroad **18+ months** who studied as a full-time student for **2 consecutive academic years** at a recognized institution, or received a degree from the institution; must return within **6 months of finishing studies / receiving the degree** ([gov.il customs guide for returning residents](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents), section "סטודנטים חוזרים", updated 18.05.2026).
   - **Important non-claim**: the gov.il customs guide does NOT distinguish between "regular returnee" and "toshav chozer vatik" (10-year) for customs purposes. The 6-year and 10-year vatik thresholds live in the income tax world (Section 14), not at Meches. Customs only knows "returnee" (2+ years abroad). Two independent CPA sources confirm there is no distinct vatik customs basket ([bshcpa - customs rights for returning residents](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/זכויות-תושבים-חוזרים-במכס/), kolzchut customs exemption page below).

2. **Household-shipment count and window (THE fact the skill must get right - returnees are NOT olim)**
   - **2 shipments maximum** for returnees (NOT 3 like olim). Exact text: "ניתן לייבא את חפצי הבית בשני משלוחים בלבד" ([gov.il customs guide ch. "חפצי בית המיובאים מחוץ לארץ"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=4), updated 18.05.2026).
   - **9 months from date of return**, NOT extendable: "תקופת הזכויות, שהיא תשעה חודשים מיום החזרה לארץ. לתשומת ליבכם: תקופה זו אינה ניתנת להארכה" (same page, same chapter).
   - The 9-month clock starts on the day of physical entry to Israel after the qualifying period abroad, not on the day the תעודת תושב חוזר is issued by Misrad HaAliyah. Surface this explicitly because most returnees confuse the two.

3. **Item caps per family**
   - **Up to 3 TV sets and 3 personal computers per family** in total ([gov.il customs guide ch. "חפצי בית"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=4), exact text: "ניתן לייבא עד 3 מקלטי טלוויזיה ו-3 מחשבים אישיים, לכל משפחה").
   - **Other electrical/electronic appliances and major furniture**: **1 unit of each type** (one fridge, one dishwasher, one washing machine, one dryer, one oven, etc.). Exact text: "מכשירי חשמל ואלקטרוניקה אחרים ופריטי ריהוט עיקריים - ניתן לייבא פריט אחד מכל סוג" (same page, same chapter).
   - **Mail-package shipment counts against the 2-shipment quota** if it contains household goods. Clothing-only postal packages within a tight window do not count: arrival within 30 days before entry / on the day of entry / within 3 months after entry ([gov.il customs guide ch. "יבוא חפצים בחבילות דואר"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=7)).

4. **Personal-effects on arrival (separate from the 2 shipments, only via accompanying luggage)**
   - Clothing, footwear, personal toiletries in customary travel quantities, free of charge ([gov.il ch. "החפצים הפטורים ממסים"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=3)).
   - **Other items up to $200 USD value per entrant** age 2+, not poolable across family members. Above $200 the entrant owes tax on the FULL value of the item, not just the excess (same chapter, exact text: "אם ערכו של חפץ מיובא עולה על 200$, אין אפשרות לקבל פטור על 200$ ולשלם מסים על ההפרש").
   - **Mixed food**: up to 3 kg total, no single food type over 1 kg, within the $200 ceiling.
   - **Alcohol / tobacco** (entrants 18+): 1 liter spirit + 2 liter wine + 250 ml alcohol perfumes + 250 g tobacco / 200 cigarettes.
   - A single household item brought as accompanying luggage on entry day does NOT count as the "one shipment". It is duty-free and outside the 2-shipment quota provided the entrant declares it on the red channel ([gov.il ch. "חפצי בית"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=4)).

5. **6-year hold / "no double-dip" rules (the trap most returnees miss)**
   - **Items released duty-free must remain for the returnee's personal use in Israel for at least 6 years**. Exact text: "חובה שכל החפצים שישוחררו לכם לפי הזכויות שלכם, יהיו לשימושכם העצמי בישראל, לתקופה של 6 שנים לפחות" ([gov.il ch. "תנאים נוספים לאישור הזכויות"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=9), updated 18.05.2026).
   - **No re-claim of same item type** unless the returnee was in Israel at least 6 years after the prior duty-free release of that item type. Exact text: "תושבים חוזרים יהיו זכאים לייבא או לרכוש בפטור ממסים, את אותו סוג חפץ ביתי, ששחררו בעבר בפטור ממסים, בתנאי ששהו בישראל במשך 6 שנים לפחות" (same chapter).
   - **Spousal anti-stacking**: a returnee cannot re-claim the same item type their spouse already released duty-free unless 6 years have passed AND they were not spouses at the time of the prior release (same chapter).
   - **"Year abroad" counting**: a single year of absence counts even if the returnee visited Israel up to 4 cumulative months in that year (same chapter, "בכל שנת היעדרות מהארץ אתם יכולים לבקר בארץ... לתקופה מצטברת של עד 4 חודשים").
   - **Insurance and personal use**: items must be insured at full value including all taxes; all items must be in the returnee's name and intended for personal use in reasonable quantities (same chapter).

6. **Vehicle import benefit (the SECOND fact the skill must get right)**
   - **There is no purchase-tax / VAT / customs-duty exemption for returnees on vehicles**. The ONLY benefit is an extended vehicle-age window: a returnee can personally import a vehicle up to **48 months old from manufacture date**, vs. **24 months** for general Israeli residents ([gov.il customs guide ch. "יבוא רכב עם מנוע"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=8), and confirmed by two CPA sources: [bshcpa "Vehicle Import" section](https://www.bshcpa.co.il/זכויות-תושבים-חוזרים-במכס/) and [kolzchut - personal vehicle import for returning residents and students](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/יבוא_אישי_של_רכב_לתושבים_וסטודנטים_חוזרים), updated 10.12.2025).
   - **The "48 months from manufacture" replaces the regular 24-month-from-manufacture cap on personal import**. The vehicle must also be a make/model approved by Misrad HaTachbura, and from the current model year or one of the 4 previous model years.
   - **Driver's license requirement**: must hold a valid Israeli driver's license for the vehicle category, age 17+ on entry day (kolzchut personal vehicle import).
   - **9-month window applies to vehicle too**: vehicle must be imported within 9 months from date of entry (kolzchut personal vehicle import).
   - **No specific quota per family** is published; sources are silent on a hard cap, suggesting the standard personal-import limits apply. Returnees should verify at Meches and Misrad HaTachbura before importing more than one vehicle.
   - **Tax math at clearance**: full purchase tax + VAT + customs duty apply at standard rates. Per gov.il personal_import_of_vehicles_guide chapter 3 (updated 15.01.2026), the COMBINED rate (customs + purchase tax + VAT, with VAT at 18% from 2025-01-01) lands at ~116% of CIF for passenger cars from FTA-origin, ~131% for non-FTA, ~75-87% for EVs, ~103-117% for collector cars. Hybrids depend on green-tax pollution grade. Equipment-level refund mechanism exists post-registration via documentation back to Meches.
   - **12-month bealut-transfer restriction**: Misrad HaTachbura imposes a 12-month ownership-transfer hold on every personally-imported vehicle, returnee or not (gov.il יבוא אישי של כלי רכב, section "מגבלת העברת בעלות"). Nahal 416 is the early-release procedure for justified cases.
   - **Plan-vs-buy economic insight**: shipping a car + paying full Israeli purchase tax + VAT often costs MORE than selling the car abroad and buying locally, because the returnee gets no tax break. The 48-month window is mainly useful when the user is bringing a desirable model unavailable in Israel or already-paid-off and wants to keep it.

7. **Forms and process to open a customs entitlement file (תיק זכאות)**
   - Apply online via the gov.il service "בקשת זכאות לפטור לתושבים חוזרים" at https://www.gov.il/he/service/request-for-entitlement-exemption-for-returning-resident-student . Exact pathway: fill טופס קביעת מעמד תושב חוזר ושליחתו באמצעות האינטרנט ([gov.il customs guide ch. "תנאים נוספים"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=9)).
   - System opens the file automatically. If automated approval fails, the returnee visits a Meches office with: their Israeli ID + passport, spouse's ID + passport, and the תעודת תושב חוזר from Misrad HaAliyah if available (same chapter).
   - Returning students bring institutional certification (2 years full-time enrollment, or degree certificate).
   - Deposit / bond: on entry the returnee may need to deposit a bond equal to 6 months of the applicable taxes; refunded if the entitlement file is approved within 9 months ([kolzchut customs exemption for returnee](https://www.kolzchut.org.il/he/פטור_ממס_קניה,_מע"מ_ותשלומי_מכס_לתושב_חוזר_ולסטודנט_חוזר_בייבוא_חפצי_בית), updated 02.03.2025).

8. **Currency declaration (anti-money-laundering, separate from customs benefits but in the same arrival flow)**
   - **50,000 NIS threshold at air/sea border**, **12,000 NIS at land borders**. Section 9 of the Anti-Money-Laundering Law requires declaration above the threshold; family members traveling together cannot pool to skirt the cap; failure to declare triggers seizure, fine, or up to 6 months imprisonment ([gov.il customs guide ch. "חובת דיווח על הכנסת כספים"](https://www.gov.il/he/pages/customs-guide-for-returning-residents?chapterIndex=10), updated 18.05.2026).

9. **Hard boundaries - Do NOT use for**
   - Process navigation, BL Form 628, kupat cholim, driver's license restoration → sister skill `israeli-returning-resident-navigator`.
   - Section 14 tax math, Form 1348, 10-year exemption → sister skill `israeli-toshav-chozer-vatik-tax-planner`.
   - Olim customs (3 shipments, bigger caps, sal-klita link, oleh-specific vehicle benefit) → sister skill `israeli-aliyah-customs-shipment-planner`.
   - People still ABROAD planning to leave Israel → `israeli-relocation-abroad`.
   - Binding customs / legal advice or actual filing - this is a planning aid; the user must engage a licensed customs broker (סוכן מכס) for the actual clearance and any complex case.

## Should cover (high-value but not strictly blocking)

- **"Ship existing car vs. sell abroad and buy locally" decision worksheet**: lay out the math (foreign book value + shipping + Israeli purchase tax + VAT + Israeli registration vs. local price) so the user can run the comparison. This is the highest-value output of the skill and the one that protects returnees from a common $5,000-$15,000 mistake.
- **The "tools of the trade" exemption** for hand-carried work tools (mentioned in ch. "כלי עבודה מהסוג הנישא ביד"). Surface as a one-line callout - returnees can bring professional hand tools duty-free in addition to the household quota; verify the current scope at gov.il before relying on it.
- **Business machines** (ch. "מכשירים ומכונות לעסק"): separate category from household goods, intended for the returnee's own business; subject to its own conditions. Mention as out-of-scope unless the returnee is bringing a meaningful piece of business equipment.
- **Vehicle equipment-level tax refund** mechanic: after clearance and Misrad HaRishui registration, the returnee can approach Meches with documentation of the actual equipment level to claim a partial refund. Refer the user to a customs broker for this; the math is non-trivial.
- **Confiscation risk for goods released within the 6-year hold period**: selling, transferring, or destroying a duty-free item during the 6-year window triggers tax payback plus interest plus a potential criminal investigation. Surface this on the timeline.
- **Restricted items requiring permits** (drones, communications gear, plants, weapons, lasers, electric scooters): handled by ch. "פריטים שהתנאי ליבוא שלהם הוא אישור הרשות המוסמכת" of the gov.il guide. Surface a short list and direct the user to the relevant authority before shipping.

## Out of scope (explicit non-goals)

- Olim chadashim 3-shipment customs basket. Customs caps for olim are larger and the sal-klita / tax basket integration is different - that lives in the planned `israeli-aliyah-customs-shipment-planner`.
- Section 14 tax mechanics. Even when the returnee is also a vatik (10-year) for Mas Hachnasa, the customs basket does not change; the tax-exemption math lives in `israeli-toshav-chozer-vatik-tax-planner`.
- Process navigation (BL Form 628, kupat cholim, license restoration, sal-klita-light grants). Sister skill `israeli-returning-resident-navigator`.
- The actual customs broker filing (Tofes 195 / declaration line items, HS codes, customs commissionership negotiations). Refer to a licensed סוכן מכס.
- Vehicle SAFETY type approval (Misrad HaTachbura testing, emissions, ESC verification) - separate process that the customs flow depends on but does not replace. Refer to a personal-import handler.
- Tax-Authority claims / disputes / appeals after clearance.

## Verification notes

- gov.il blocks WebFetch with HTTP 403. All gov.il quotes were captured via Playwright MCP rendering each `?chapterIndex=N` URL of the customs guide.
- The 48-month vehicle age window and the absence of a returnee purchase-tax exemption are corroborated by two independent CPA sources (bshcpa, kolzchut) on top of the kolzchut vehicle page that itself cites the regulations. This is the load-bearing fact the skill must NOT get wrong.
- The 9-month window, 2-shipment cap, 3-TV / 3-PC / 1-per-other-appliance caps, and 6-year hold period all come from the same gov.il customs guide page that was updated 18.05.2026 - the freshest possible source.
- The kolzchut customs page mentions a 6-months-of-applicable-taxes deposit on entry that is refunded if the entitlement file is approved within 9 months. This is operational detail, not a hard requirement to recite; surface it as a "may apply" callout.
- The skill must explicitly warn: returnees often confuse themselves with olim because both flows are run by Meches under the same broad "personal import" umbrella. The single most common mistake is assuming the 3-shipment olim basket applies. It does not. Hard, repeated callout.
