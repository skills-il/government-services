# Toshav Chozer: Customs and Personal Import Rules

This reference covers what a returning Israeli resident (toshav chozer) can bring back duty-free or with special benefits, and what they cannot.

## Who Qualifies

Two tiers of returning resident, defined by length of foreign residency:

| Status | Minimum years abroad | Main tax benefit |
|--------|---------------------|------------------|
| Toshav Chozer (regular) | 6 consecutive years | 5-year exemption on foreign-source passive income; 10-year exemption on gains from certain foreign financial assets |
| Toshav Chozer Vatik (veteran) | 10 consecutive years | 10-year exemption on all foreign-source income and gains under Section 14 of the Income Tax Ordinance |

Note: customs benefits below apply to both tiers equally -- they are about physical goods, not income.

## Household Goods and Personal Effects

Returning residents can import their household goods (raitot habayit) and personal effects duty-free, subject to:

- Items must be for personal use, not for sale or commercial use
- Reasonable quantities -- typically a single household's worth of furniture, appliances, clothing, books, art, and kitchenware
- Some items have numerical caps (e.g., appliance per type: one refrigerator, one oven, one washer, one dryer, one air conditioner per room)
- Electronics that are similar to items commonly sold commercially may trigger scrutiny

Submit a customs declaration (haktzaa) with a detailed packing list. Most relocation companies handle this as part of the shipping service.

## Vehicle Import

Returning residents can personally import one vehicle under relaxed rules compared to regular residents:

| Rule | Regular resident | Toshav chozer |
|------|------------------|---------------|
| Vehicle age (months from manufacturing) | Max 24 months | Max 48 months |
| Timing | At any time | Within 9 months of entering Israel |
| Number of vehicles | One per personal import event | One per returning resident |
| Purchase tax and VAT | Pay in full | **Pay in full** -- no exemption |

**Critical:** The toshav chozer vehicle benefit is NOT a tax exemption. It is only a relaxed age window. You pay the full Israeli purchase tax (mas kniya) and VAT on the vehicle's declared value plus shipping and insurance to Israeli port. Budget accordingly.

## Paperwork Sequence for Personal Import

A typical sequence for importing personal effects and a vehicle:

1. Before shipping: obtain a certificate of returning resident status from Misrad HaKlita (or the relevant authority) to present to customs
2. Prepare a packing list (reshimat tichanim) in English or Hebrew for all container contents
3. Obtain the vehicle's original title and export certificate from the foreign country
4. Ship via a licensed international mover; most charge extra for handling Israeli customs
5. On arrival in Israel, submit the customs declaration at the port (Haifa or Ashdod)
6. Pay any assessed taxes on items outside the personal-use definition and on the vehicle (full taxes)
7. Vehicle registration at Misrad HaRishui with the import documents and customs clearance

## Timing Gotchas

- The 9-month vehicle window starts the day you physically enter Israel for the return, not the day your shipment arrives. Ship the car early
- Missing the 9-month window means the vehicle is treated as a regular personal import, and the 48-month age limit drops to 24 months
- Rules can change between the year you plan your return and the year you actually return -- verify the current rules before committing to a ship date
