# Bituach Leumi for Israelis Living Abroad

This reference covers the practical rules for Israeli tax residents living abroad: who owes what, how much, and how to handle gaps.

## Who Must Pay

An Israeli tax resident owes Bituach Leumi (National Insurance) contributions and health insurance contributions every month, regardless of where they physically live. The driver is tax residency status, not physical location.

If you formally cut residency (see the main SKILL.md Step 2), you stop owing Bituach Leumi -- but you also lose your kupat cholim (HMO) membership and restart a waiting period when you return.

## Contribution Components

Two separate components are both collected by Bituach Leumi:

| Component | What it funds | Rate on reported income |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------------|
| National Insurance (dmei bituach leumi) | Unemployment, maternity, disability, old-age pension, child allowance, work injury | 7% above the reduced-rate ceiling, lower rate below |
| Health Insurance (dmei bituach briut) | Funds the universal health basket delivered via the four kupot cholim | 5% above the reduced-rate ceiling, lower rate below |

## 2026 Minimums (effective 01.01.2026)

For an Israeli resident abroad with no reported income, the minimum monthly payment depends on whether they live in a treaty country:

- **Treaty country (USA, UK, most of EU, Canada), paying social security there: 123 NIS per month (health insurance only)**
- **Non-treaty country, no reported income: 266 NIS per month combined (National Insurance + health insurance)**

Source: Bituach Leumi publication "שינוי בתשלום דמי ביטוח לאומי ודמי ביטוח בריאות לשנת 2026". Amounts are indexed annually; verify each year at btl.gov.il.

If you report income from abroad (e.g., salary from a foreign employer), contributions are calculated on that reported income at the rates above, subject to the annual ceiling (the same ceiling that applies domestically -- set by the Ministry of Finance based on average wage).

## Treaty Country Exemption

Israel has bilateral social security agreements with many countries. If you live in a treaty country AND pay social security there on employment income, you are exempt from Israeli National Insurance on that same income. You still owe health insurance (dmei briut) in Israel.

Treaty countries include (non-exhaustive): United States, United Kingdom, Germany, France, Netherlands, Belgium, Sweden, Denmark, Finland, Norway, Switzerland, Austria, Italy, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Bulgaria, Romania, Canada, Uruguay. Check the Bituach Leumi international agreements page for the current list and the specific rules per treaty.

Non-treaty countries (e.g., Singapore, UAE, most of Asia): you may owe full Israeli contributions on reported income.

## How to Set Up Payments Before Leaving

The cleanest setup, done before departure:

1. Notify Bituach Leumi that you are moving abroad but remain an Israeli tax resident. Use the "Declaration of Residence" form (Hodaa al Makom Megurim) or update through the personal area (ezor ishi) at btl.gov.il
2. Provide the foreign address for correspondence
3. Open a standing order (horaat keva) from your Israeli bank to Bituach Leumi for the minimum health insurance amount (and National Insurance if non-treaty country)
4. Verify the standing order is active by logging in to the personal area a month after departure

## Catching Up on Missed Payments

If you discover you owe back payments after months or years abroad:

1. Log in to btl.gov.il personal area and request a balance statement (ishur yitra)
2. The balance will show the outstanding amount and any interest or indexation (hizmada v'ribit)
3. Pay in one lump or request a payment plan (pririsa) for larger amounts
4. Once cleared, verify that kupat cholim coverage is active (no waiting period flag)
5. Set up the standing order to prevent future gaps

## Common Mistakes

- Assuming "I live abroad so I don't owe anything" -- residency status is the test, not physical location
- Cancelling the kupat cholim thinking it is a savings move -- you cannot "cancel" it while Israeli tax resident, the linkage is automatic, and the wrong action is simply to stop paying
- Not noting the treaty country rule and paying double (NI both in the foreign country and in Israel)
- Forgetting to update the foreign address, missing payment notices, and accruing interest on unpaid amounts
