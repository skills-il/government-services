# Return-Side Deep Topics

Detailed coverage of three areas that come up for relocators planning a return to Israel: stock options earned partly abroad, voluntary Bituach Leumi continuation, and dual-jurisdiction estate planning. The summaries in `SKILL.md` Step 6 point here for full mechanics.

## Stock options earned abroad (RSU / Section 102)

A returnee (especially Toshav Chozer Vatik with 10+ years abroad) holding stock options or RSUs that vested partly during the foreign-residency period and partly after the return faces split-period treatment:

- The portion that vested on work performed abroad (during foreign tax residency) is foreign-source ordinary income, exempt under Section 14 for vatik returnees during the 10-year window.
- The portion that vested on work performed in Israel (after the return) is Israeli-source ordinary income, taxable at the marginal rate.

Sourcing is by where the work was performed during the vesting period, NOT by grant date. A US RSU granted in 2022, vesting over 4 years, where the holder returned to Israel in 2024: the 2022-2024 portion is foreign-source (exempt for vatik), the 2024-2026 portion is Israeli-source (taxable).

Engage a CPA with a workdays-by-location log; this is the single biggest "I didn't know I owed" surprise for returning tech workers. Full mechanics in the sister skill `israeli-toshav-chozer-vatik-tax-planner`.

## Voluntary Bituach Leumi (Bituach mi-Ratzon)

Self-employed Israelis abroad can opt into voluntary continuation of specific BL branches (most relevantly: maternity/parental coverage, work-injury) that ordinarily do NOT cover work performed abroad. Eligibility and rates are set by BL and depend on past contribution history.

The main use case is a returnee who plans to need unemployment benefits on re-entry. Eligibility for unemployment benefits depends on a qualifying contribution history (12 of the last 18 months for full eligibility), so voluntary contributions while abroad can preserve that window.

Check the Kol-Zchut "ביטוח לאומי מרצון" page or call BL *6050 before assuming you are covered. Rates change annually with the BL minimums.

## Estate planning while abroad

Israelis with assets in both Israel and the host country face dual-jurisdiction estate planning:

- An Israeli will (tzava'ah) is valid in Israel for Israeli-situated assets (real estate, bank accounts, securities held with Israeli brokers). It may or may not be recognized in the host country.
- A host-country will is valid for assets in that country.
- The cleanest setup is two coordinated wills, one per jurisdiction, drafted by lawyers who consult each other to avoid contradictions.
- Israel has no inheritance tax (mas yerusha), but most host countries do (US estate tax on US-situated assets above $60K for non-resident-alien decedents; UK inheritance tax; French succession). Plan around the host country's regime, not Israel's.
- Power of attorney (yipui koach) drafted in Israel may need apostille and translation to be honored in the host country, and vice versa.

This is real planning, not deferrable hygiene, for relocations exceeding 5 years.
