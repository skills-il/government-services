# Returning-Resident Employment, Education, and Benefits Basket

This reference expands on Step 3 of the main SKILL.md. Consult per topic.

## Vocational Training Vouchers

- Cover professional and vocational training costs (course fees, certification, materials)
- Require Ulpan Aleph proficiency at minimum (formal exception for learning disabilities)
- Subject to district budget availability; apply through the local Misrad HaAliyah employment counselor (yo'etz ta'asuka)
- Best path: take the eligibility assessment in month 1-2 before committing to a training program

## Salary Assistance

- Up to **24 months** from the Toshav Chozer status date
- For ages 18 through retirement age
- Professional athletes and coaches with academic degrees explicitly eligible
- Application through Misrad HaAliyah employment counselor
- Paid as a top-up on Israeli employment salary, not as a separate cash grant

## Ma'alot Innovation Program (entrepreneurs)

- **5 business centers nationwide** (Tel Aviv, Jerusalem, Haifa, Be'er Sheva, and one additional region; locations rotate with budget cycles, verify at application time)
- **NIS 250 fee for business-model creation**; all other services (mentorship, market research support, regulatory navigation) are free
- Eligibility differs by return-date cohort due to the April 2020 reform:
  - **Pre-March 31, 2020 returnees**: 2-year application window, 3+ years abroad required
  - **Post-April 1, 2020 returnees**: 2-year application window, 5+ years abroad required
- Application also opens additional funding tracks (Innovation Authority grants, Misrad HaCalcala loan-guarantee programs)

## Guaranteed Income Support (Havtachat Hachnasa) for returnees

| Household | Monthly threshold |
|---|---|
| Individual | NIS 2,680 |
| Couple | NIS 3,694 |
| Family of 3 | NIS 4,106 |
| Family of 4+ | NIS 4,836 |

- Available up to **3 months within the first year** of return
- Extendable through the end of the year if the returnee is employed but earns below minimum wage with salary slip (slope-out provision)
- Filed at Misrad HaAliyah, NOT at Bituach Leumi (unlike the standard Israeli Havtachat Hachnasa for non-returnees)
- Counts as taxable income; coordinate with Teum Mas

## Lone Soldier (Chayal Boded) for returnees

Returnees aged 17-28 who left Israel as minors may be drafted for full IDF service on return. The IDF Meitav unit determines status. Once drafted, "Chayal Boded" status carries a named legal benefit basket:

- **IDF salary supplement** (additional monthly grant beyond standard compulsory-service pay)
- **Misrad HaAliyah supplementary grant** if IDF conscription occurs within 5 years of Toshav Chozer status
- **21 days annual overseas-parents leave** with IDF-covered round-trip travel
- **Housing subsidy** for soldiers without parents in Israel
- **IDF Special Fund** for exceptional hardship cases (medical, family emergency, housing crisis)
- **Family payments** (mishpacha mefarnesset) if household income meets thresholds
- Misrad HaBitachon end-of-service grant on discharge

Apply for Chayal Boded recognition through the unit's social worker (mashakit tash); separately, Misrad HaAliyah's national-service coordinator (Natalie Furman, 03-9733333) handles the ministry-side supplementary grant for both IDF and Sherut Leumi paths.

## National Service (Sherut Leumi) for returnees

- Service must start within **2 years** of Toshav Chozer status
- **Parents must be abroad** at the time of service to qualify for the supplementary grant
- Required documents: acceptance confirmation from a recognized Sherut Leumi placement (or Sherut Ezrachi), valid Teudat Zehut, bank account details
- **Coordinator: Natalie Furman**, National Soldiers Coordinator at Misrad HaAliyah, main line **03-9733333**

## Education reinforcement for returnee children

- Eligibility: child was previously an Israeli citizen AND completed at least **4 years of academic studies outside Israel**
- Entitled to: additional weekly reinforcement sessions (subject allocation, typically Hebrew, math, Tanach), potential matriculation exam accommodations (modified time, oral exam in mother tongue for select subjects)
- Apply at the school's חינוך coordinator or the local municipality education department within the first month of enrollment
- Distinct from the "Da Ivrit" Hebrew reinforcement program, which is available without the 4-year academic threshold

## Returning Artist track

- Ages 17+ for the returnee themselves; 18+ for children of a Toshav Chozer applying as artists
- Process:
  1. Consult a Misrad HaAliyah employment counselor (yo'etz ta'asuka)
  2. Complete the "Application Form to the Professional Committees" (טופס בקשה לוועדות המקצועיות)
  3. Submit: CV, education certificates, professional recommendations, an employment certificate (if currently employed in the field), and a consent form allowing Misrad HaAliyah to contact the employer
- Recognition unlocks Ministry integration assistance specifically in the artistic field (placement support, residency program connections, occasional grants)

## Returning Scientist track

- Eligibility: at least 5 years abroad as a researcher (academic or industry R&D), no more than 1 year back in Israel at the time of application
- **Age cap: 37** for the Research Scholarship program
- Apply through the Center for Integration in Science (CIS, מרכז לקליטה במדע) at Misrad HaAliyah
- Provides: salary supplements at Israeli academic institutions, relocation grants, lab-setup grants for senior researchers, fellowship matching with Israeli universities

## El Al returnee-airfare benefit

- Discounted El Al fare with expanded luggage allowance for the qualifying return flight
- Booked through El Al's returning-resident channel BEFORE purchasing the flight, not retroactively
- Requires preliminary Misrad HaAliyah eligibility confirmation (the "zachaut ikronit" check, NOT the full certificate)
- One round-trip per family member; coordinate the flight date with the Misrad HaAliyah 24-month clock start
